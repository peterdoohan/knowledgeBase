### One-line summary

Phosphorylation of connexin43 at serine-365 acts as a gatekeeper event that induces a conformational change in the C-terminal domain, prevents subsequent PKC-dependent phosphorylation at S368, and maintains gap junction channel conductance.

---

### Background & motivation

Gap junction intercellular communication is regulated by connexin phosphorylation, which modulates channel assembly, gating, and turnover. Cx43 undergoes phosphorylation at multiple serine residues throughout its life cycle, with specific phosphorylation events correlated with changes in localization and channel function. Myocardial ischemia causes Cx43 dephosphorylation and loss of intercalated disk localization, contributing to arrhythmias. While phosphorylation at S368 by PKC reduces channel conductance, the regulatory mechanisms controlling this modification remain incompletely understood.

### Methods

- **Mass spectrometry**: LC/ESI MS/MS analysis of trypsin-digested Cx43 immunoprecipitated from NRK cells to identify phosphorylation sites
- **Cell culture**: MDCK cells (Cx43-deficient) stably transfected with wild-type, S365A, or S365D mutant Cx43; NRK cells with endogenous Cx43
- **Antibody generation**: Custom polyclonal phosphospecific antibody (pS365) raised against phosphorylated peptide CQRPS(pS)RASS
- **Western blotting**: Fluorescence-based co-labeling with total Cx43 antibody (NT1) and phosphospecific antibodies
- **Immunofluorescence**: Confocal microscopy of Cx43 localization using pS365 and total Cx43 antibodies
- **NMR spectroscopy**: 15N-HSQC experiments comparing wild-type and S365D mutant Cx43CT (amino acids 255–382) at pH 5.8 and 7.5
- **In vitro phosphorylation**: PKC-mediated phosphorylation of GST-Cx43CT fusion proteins with γ-[32P]ATP and detection by autoradiography/scintillation counting
- **Dye microinjection**: Gap junctional communication assay comparing wild-type and S365A mutant cells
- **Immunohistochemistry**: Mouse heart tissue sections stained for Cx43, pS365, and pS368 under normoxic and hypoxic conditions

---

### Key findings

- **S365 phosphorylation identified**: LC/MS/MS confirmed Cx43 is phosphorylated at S365 in cultured NRK cells; identified via neutral-loss fragmentation pattern of tryptic peptide V347-R366
- **Phosphospecific antibody validation**: pS365 antibody specifically recognized wild-type Cx43 but not S365A (non-phosphorylatable) or S365D (phosphomimetic) mutants; antibody preferentially labeled P1 and P2 isoforms
- **S365 required for P1 isoform**: S365A and S365D mutants failed to produce the P1 isoform; S364A mutant could still produce P1, indicating S365-specific requirement
- **Structural conformational change**: NMR 15N-HSQC showed S365D mutation caused dramatic shifts in residues T275-A276, G285-Y286, L356-S368, and R370-D379; effects propagated nearly 100 residues upstream, indicating global conformational change
- **Gap junction localization**: pS365 immunofluorescence showed phosphorylation present at cell-cell junctions in cultured cells and intercalated disks in heart tissue; staining lost during hypoxia when Cx43 redistributed to lateral membranes
- **Inverse relationship with S368**: S365 phosphorylation was constitutive in normoxic heart but lost during hypoxia; conversely, S368 phosphorylation increased 1.8-fold during hypoxia (P = 0.04)
- **S365 phosphorylation prevents S368 phosphorylation**: Immunoprecipitation with pS365 antibody yielded Cx43 with minimal S368 phosphorylation; PMA-induced S368 phosphorylation was enhanced 2–3-fold in S365A mutants but only 0.3-fold in S365D mutants
- **PKC inhibition by S365 phosphorylation**: In vitro PKC phosphorylation of GST-Cx43CT was 4.3-fold more efficient on wild-type than S365D mutant (P < 0.0001, n = 4)
- **Functional gap junction communication**: S365A mutant cells showed normal dye transfer (31.6 ± 7.0 cells per injection) comparable to wild-type (30.7 ± 3.5), indicating S365 phosphorylation not essential for basic channel function

### Prevailing model of the system under study

The prevailing model held that Cx43 phosphorylation regulates gap junction assembly, gating, and turnover throughout the connexin life cycle. Multiple phosphorylation sites on the C-terminal tail were known to produce distinct SDS-PAGE isoforms (P0, P1, P2), with specific sites correlated with functional states. Phosphorylation at S368 by PKC was established as a channel-regulatory event that reduces conductance, particularly during ischemia, cell cycle progression, and upon phorbol ester treatment. The structural mechanisms linking specific phosphorylation events to functional outcomes were poorly understood, as was the potential interdependence of phosphorylation sites.

---

### What this paper contributes

This paper establishes phosphorylation at S365 as a constitutive, gatekeeper event that structurally primes Cx43 and prevents downstream PKC-dependent phosphorylation at S368. The key advances include:

- **Identification of S365 as a phosphorylation site**: Mass spectrometry confirmed S365 phosphorylation in homeostatic cells and heart tissue, establishing it as a constitutive modification in the Cx43 life cycle.

- **Structural mechanism**: NMR demonstrated that phosphorylation (mimicked by S365D) induces a global conformational change in the C-terminal domain, affecting residues up to 100 amino acids away. This conformational switch provides a structural basis for the P1 isoform and altered protein-protein interactions.

- **Hierarchical phosphorylation**: S365 phosphorylation prevents PKC-mediated phosphorylation at S368 by inducing a conformational state that blocks the kinase. This establishes a phosphorylation hierarchy where S365 must be dephosphorylated before S368 can be modified.

- **Ischemic relevance**: In heart tissue, S365 phosphorylation is constitutive at intercalated disks but lost during hypoxia, coinciding with S368 phosphorylation increase and Cx43 redistribution. This identifies S365 dephosphorylation as a key ischemic event that enables subsequent channel downregulation.

### Mechanistic insight

This paper provides mechanistic insight at the molecular and biochemical level, though it does not meet the strict bar for Marr's three levels as defined in the instructions (the paper does not present a formal algorithm that could explain a neural phenomenon, nor does it provide neural data in the sense of electrophysiology from neurons).

However, the paper does provide substantial mechanistic insight at the protein level:

**Molecular mechanism**: The study establishes that phosphorylation at S365 serves as a gatekeeper for subsequent PKC-dependent phosphorylation at S368. NMR structural data reveal that the S365D phosphomimetic mutation induces a stable conformational change in the C-terminal domain that propagates up to 100 residues away. This conformational switch explains why S365 phosphorylation blocks PKC access to S368.

**Hierarchical phosphorylation network**: The results demonstrate that phosphorylation events on Cx43 are not independent but follow a specific sequence: S365 must be dephosphorylated before S368 can be phosphorylated by PKC. This hierarchical regulation provides a mechanism for switching between channel states.

**Physiological relevance**: In cardiac ischemia, the loss of S365 phosphorylation coincides with increased S368 phosphorylation and gap junction redistribution, identifying S365 dephosphorylation as a key molecular event in ischemic uncoupling.

### Limitations & open questions

- **Phosphomimetic limitations**: The S365D mutation does not perfectly replicate phosphorylation (aspartate has different steric properties and charge distribution than phosphoserine), and low-level S368 phosphorylation was still detected in S365D mutants.
- **Structural resolution**: NMR studies were limited to the C-terminal domain (amino acids 255–382), not the full-length protein, so the effects on the complete connexin structure remain inferred.
- **In vivo dynamics**: The temporal sequence of S365 dephosphorylation relative to S368 phosphorylation during ischemia was not definitively established—whether dephosphorylation strictly precedes or merely enables subsequent phosphorylation.
- **Functional consequences**: While S365 phosphorylation prevents S368 phosphorylation and channel conductance reduction, the direct effect of S365 phosphorylation alone on channel gating was not fully characterized.
- **Phosphatase identity**: The phosphatase(s) responsible for S365 dephosphorylation during ischemia were not identified.
