### One-line summary

PMA-induced PKC activation increases proenkephalin mRNA expression in rat astrocytes via de novo synthesized AP-1 transcription factors (c-Fos, Fra-1, c-Jun), not via phospho-CREB.

### Background & motivation

Glial cells express proenkephalin (proENK) mRNA and its protein products, but the molecular mechanisms regulating proENK gene expression in astrocytes remain poorly characterized. Previous studies showed that second messenger systems (cAMP, PKC activation, calcium channel depolarization) can activate proENK gene expression, and that proto-oncogene products (Fos and Jun family proteins) may serve as transcriptional regulators through AP-1 DNA binding domains. This study investigates the specific involvement of proto-oncogenes and CREB in PKC-mediated proENK gene regulation in primary cultured rat astrocytes.

### Methods

- **Cell culture**: Primary astrocyte cultures prepared from 1-day-old rat (Sprague-Dawley) neonate cortex; cells grown 14–15 days to form dense monolayers (>95% type I astrocytes as confirmed by GFAP-positive, A2B5-negative staining)
- **Treatments**: PMA (2.5 μM, PKC activator) for 0.5–4 h; cycloheximide (CHX, 15 μM, protein synthesis inhibitor) pre-treatment 30 min before PMA
- **RNA analysis**: Non-isotope Northern blot with digoxigenin-labeled cRNA probes for proENK, c-fos, c-jun, fra-1; cyclophilin as loading control
- **Protein analysis**: Western immunoblot for proENK protein (using anti-MERF antibody), c-Fos, Fra-1, c-Jun, CREB, and phospho-CREB (Ser133)
- **DNA binding assays**: Electrophoretic mobility shift assay (EMSA) for AP-1 and ENKCRE-2 DNA binding activities; self- and cross-competition with unlabeled probes
- **Quantification**: Bio-profil Bio-1D application for mRNA quantification; Student's paired t-test for statistical comparison

### Key findings

- **ProENK mRNA induction**: PMA (2.5 μM) increased proENK mRNA ~5.4-fold at 4 h; this increase was attenuated by CHX pre-treatment (to ~1.57-fold), indicating requirement for de novo protein synthesis
- **ProENK protein unchanged**: Intracellular proENK protein levels showed no significant change with PMA or CHX treatment, suggesting rapid processing or secretion of newly synthesized proENK
- **Proto-oncogene mRNA dynamics**: PMA transiently increased c-fos and c-jun mRNA (peaking at 0.5–1 h, reduced by 4 h); fra-1 mRNA showed sustained increase (beginning at 1 h, continuing to 4 h)
- **CHX differential effects on proto-oncogenes**: CHX pre-treatment enhanced PMA-induced c-fos mRNA at 1 h; did not affect c-jun; inhibited fra-1 at early time points (0.5–1 h) but enhanced it at 4 h
- **Proto-oncoprotein induction**: Western blot showed PMA increased c-Fos and c-Jun proteins at 0.5 h with further enhancement at 4 h; Fra-1 protein increased only at 4 h; all increases were inhibited by CHX pre-treatment
- **Phospho-CREB dynamics**: PMA transiently increased phospho-CREB at 0.5–1 h (returning to baseline by 4 h); CHX pre-treatment further enhanced this phospho-CREB increase; no correlation with proENK mRNA induction
- **DNA binding activities**: Both AP-1 and ENKCRE-2 DNA binding activities increased beginning at 0.5 h, peaking at 1–4 h; these increases were inhibited by CHX; cross-competition showed AP-1 proteins participate in ENKCRE-2 binding
- **Mechanistic interpretation**: The correlation between proENK mRNA induction and AP-1/ENKCRE-2 DNA binding activities (both CHX-sensitive) suggests that newly synthesized AP-1 proteins (c-Fos, Fra-1, c-Jun) are the key transcriptional regulators; phospho-CREB appears not to be directly involved but may act indirectly via c-fos induction

### Prevailing model of the system under study

The prevailing model, as presented in the introduction, held that: (1) glial cells express proenkephalin (proENK) mRNA and its protein products; (2) proENK gene expression is regulated by second messenger systems including cAMP, protein kinase C (PKC) activation, and calcium channel depolarization; (3) these short-term second messenger responses lead to prolonged gene activation via "third messenger" proteins including proto-oncogene products (Fos and Jun family proteins) that bind to AP-1 and AP-1-like DNA domains in promoter regions; (4) cAMP response element binding protein (CREB) can also regulate gene expression through phosphorylation without altering DNA binding activity. However, the specific involvement of these mechanisms in proENK gene regulation in primary cultured rat astrocytes was not well characterized.

### What this paper contributes

This paper establishes that in primary cultured rat astrocytes: (1) PKC activation by PMA increases proENK mRNA expression (~5.4-fold at 4 h) via a mechanism requiring de novo protein synthesis; (2) the transcription factors c-Fos, Fra-1, and c-Jun are induced by PMA and are likely the key regulators of proENK gene expression, as evidenced by the correlation between their induction and AP-1/ENKCRE-2 DNA binding activities; (3) phospho-CREB, while transiently activated by PMA, is not directly involved in proENK gene regulation (no correlation with proENK mRNA induction, and CHX actually enhances phospho-CREB while attenuating proENK mRNA); (4) AP-1 proteins can bind to both AP-1 and ENKCRE-2 sites, as shown by cross-competition experiments. These findings provide a mechanistic framework for understanding how PKC activation leads to proENK gene expression in astrocytes, highlighting the central role of AP-1 family transcription factors.

### Limitations & open questions

- **Cell type specificity**: The requirement for ongoing protein synthesis in proENK mRNA expression may differ between primary cultured astrocytes and transformed cell lines (e.g., C6 glioma cells showed different responses); the generalizability to other glial cell types or in vivo conditions is uncertain
- **Protein vs. mRNA disconnect**: Intracellular proENK protein levels did not change despite mRNA induction, suggesting rapid processing or secretion, but the exact fate of newly synthesized proENK was not determined
- **Incomplete mechanistic mapping**: The study did not test all possible proto-oncoproteins that might contribute to AP-1 and ENKCRE-2 DNA binding activities; post-translational modifications of transcription factors were not fully characterized
- **Indirect CREB role**: While phospho-CREB was ruled out as a direct regulator of proENK, its potential indirect role via c-fos induction was hypothesized but not definitively established
- **AP-2 domain not examined**: The human proENK promoter contains an AP-2 domain that can confer phorbol ester responsiveness, but this was not investigated in the rat astrocyte system
- **CHX off-target effects**: Cycloheximide may have signaling effects beyond protein synthesis inhibition, complicating interpretation of some results
