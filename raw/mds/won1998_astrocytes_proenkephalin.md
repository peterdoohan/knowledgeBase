The stimulation of rat astrocytes with phorbol-12-myristate-13-acetate increases the proenkephalin mRNA: involvement of proto-oncogenes 

Je-Seong Won, Dong-Keun Song, Yung-Hi Kim, Sung-Oh Huh, Hong-Won Suh[)] _Department of Pharmacology and Institute of Natural Medicine, College of Medicine, Hallym Uni_ Õ _ersity, 1 Okchun-Dong, Chunchon, Kangwon-Do, 200-702, South Korea_ Accepted 21 October 1997 

Je-Seong Won, Dong-Keun Song, Yung-Hi Kim, Sung-Oh Huh, Hong-Won Suh 

## **Abstract** 

The effect of phorbol-12-myristate-13-acetate ŽPMA. on the regulation of proenkephalin ŽproENK. mRNA level, ENKCRE-2 or AP-1 DNA binding activity, and the mRNA and protein levels of proto-oncogenes Žc- _fos_ , _fra-1_ , and c- _jun_ . in primary cultured rat astrocytes were studied. The proENK mRNA level was elevated at 4 h after the treatment of PMA Ž2.5 mM. without altering the intracellular proENK protein level, and this increase was attenuated by pre-treatment with cycloheximide ŽCHX; 15 mM., a protein synthesis inhibitor. Both AP-1 and ENKCRE-2 DNA binding activities were markedly increased at 1–4 h by PMA treatment and these PMA-induced responses were inhibited by pre-treatment with CHX, showing that the increase of proENK mRNA level was well correlated with the AP-1 and ENKCRE-2 DNA binding activities. In contrast, although the phospho-CREBP level was also increased by PMA at 0.5–1 h, the pre-treatment with CHX further increased the PMA-induced phospho-CREBP level. In addition, PMA caused the induction of c- _fos_ , c- _jun_ and _fra-1_ mRNA level and, especially, PMA-induced increase of _fra-1_ mRNA level was further enhanced by CHX treatment at 4 h. Furthermore, western immunoblot assay showed that PMA caused induction of c-Fos, Fra-1, and c-Jun protein levels. PMA-induced increases of proto-oncoproteins levels were also inhibited by CHX treatment. The results suggest that newly synthesized AP-1 proteins, such as c-Fos, Fra-1, and c-Jun may play important roles in the regulation of PMA-induced proENK gene expression in cultured rat astrocytes. Phospho-CREB protein appears not to be involved in the regulation of PMA-induced proENK gene expression. q 1998 Elsevier Science B.V.

## **1. Introduction** 

Glial cells execute essential functions in central nervous system ŽCNS. development and are also believed to play important roles during gliosis in response to trauma or disease w3 .x These developmental and pathological states have also been associated with CNS expression of opioid genes w36,49 .x Previous studies have reported that glial cells express proenkephalin ŽproENK. mRNA and its protein product w19,31,40,43,50,56 .x However, the molecular mechanisms underlying the regulation of proENK gene expression in rat glial cells have not been well characterized. 

In several tissues and cells, the proENK gene expression appears to be an important participant in ligand-medi- 

ated signal transduction events, such as an increase in intracellular cyclic AMP ŽcAMP. levels w12,45,53,54 ,x the activation of protein kinase C ŽPKC. w52 ,x and the depolarization of voltage-dependent calcium channels w22,32,41 .x These short-term responses which were evoked by ligand mediated signal transduction are followed by a more prolonged effect on proENK gene activation. Several classes of cellular and nuclear proteins that serve as the third messengers are rapidly and transiently up-regulated by the second messenger system, and involved in the regulation of specific gene expression such as proENK gene w4,10,26,38 .x 

Previous reports have demonstrated that several types of proto-oncogene products, such as Fos and Jun family proteins, may serve as the transcriptional regulators in the expressions of various genes, as well as proENK gene, through the interaction with AP-1 and AP-1 like DNA domains in their promoter regions w4,10,26,38 .x In addition, cAMP response element binding protein ŽCREBP. would also be expected to alter the expression of specific

## _2.2. Isolation of total RNA and proteins_ 

Total cellular RNAs were extracted from primary cultured astrocytes using a rapid guanidine thiocyanate–water saturated phenolrchloroform extraction and subsequent precipitation with acidic sodium acetate w8 .x Total cellular 

RNA in the aqueous phase were precipitated with cold isopropyl alcohol. Isolated RNA samples were subjected to spectrophotometric analysis at 260 nm and 280 nm. The separated organic layer was extracted twice with an equal volume of sterile Millipore water and proteins were precipitated by adding 2 volumes of absolute ethanol to the water-extracted organic phase. The protein pellets were washed twice with cold absolute ethanol and dried. The dried pellets were dissolved in a denaturing buffer w6 M guanidium chloride, 20 mM Tris–HCl ŽpH 8.0., and 1 mM EDTA .x The protein samples were dialyzed against a renaturing buffer Ž20 mM Tris–HCl ŽpH 8.0., 100 mM NaCl, 1 mM EDTA, 5 mM dithiothreitol, 5 mM MgCl 2 , 0.4 mM phenylmethylsulfonyl fluoride, and 20% glycerol. at 48C. ## **4. Discussion** 

We examined the effect of PMA Ža protein kinase C activator. on proENK mRNA expression in primary cultured neonate rat astrocytes and found that, although an increase of proENK mRNA could not be observed until 0.5–1 h after PMA treatment, proENK mRNA level was increased 5.4-fold above the control level after 4 h incubation with PMA ŽFig. 1A.. This finding is partially in line with a report by Theodoridu et al. w47x in that activation of protein kinase C pathway is able to increase proENK mRMA expression in primary cultured astrocytes. However, the level of proENK mRNA was not altered in C6 rat glioma cells when protein kinase C pathway was stimulated w30 . The differential regulatory mechanisms involvedx in the proENK mRNA expression between primary cultured astrocytes and transformed C6 rat glioma cells should be further investigated. 

Induction of proENK mRNA level by PMA was attenuated by the pre-treatment of CHX. Thus, the protein de novo synthesis is required for the increase of proENK mRNA level induced by the activation of PKC pathway in cultured rat astrocytes. In previous work in bovine adrenal chromaffin cells, Mar et al. w29x also reported that phorbol ester increases proENK mRNA level and PMA-induced response was blocked by CHX. Additionally, we have recently reported that the increased hippocampal proENK 

mRNA level induced by kainic acid is blocked by the pre-treatment with CHX in vivo experiment w51 .x However, in C6 rat glioma cells, the increase of proENK mRNA level induced by the activation adenylate cyclase by norepinephrine was not changed by the pre-treatment with CHX w30,53 .x These results imply that the requirement of on-going protein synthesis for the proENK mRNA expression may be dependent on tissue or cell specificity. In the Western immuno-blot analysis, any remarkable change of intracellular proENK protein level was not observed by PMA treatment ŽFig. 1B.. Although the levels of smaller proENK-derived peptides such as Leu- and Met-enkephalin in the cells and medium were not examined, it is assumed that the newly synthesized proENK protein may be rapidly processed into smaller enkephalincontaining peptides or released into extracellular space. Our result is similar to in vitro studies of the cultured astrocytes, C6 rat glioma cells, and bovine adrenal chromaffin cells w2,29,30 .x 

We examined the effect of PMA on proto-oncogenes and found the mRNA levels of c- _fos_ and c- _jun_ were only increased at 0.5–1 h and reduced 4 h after PMA treatment ŽFig. 2.. However, the _fra-1_ mRNA level began to increase 1 h and continuously increased up to 4 h after PMA treatment. This finding indicates that the regulatory mechanism underlying PMA-induced mRNA expression of each proto-oncogene may be different. In support of this hypothesis, the PMA-induced c- _fos_ , _fra-1_ and c- _jun_ mRNA levels were also differentially affected by pre-treatment with CHX. The pre-treatment of CHX further enhanced the c- _fos_ mRNA level induced by PMA at 1 h while CHX treatment did not affect the PMA-induced increased c- _jun_ mRNA. Furthermore, PMA-induced _fra-1_ mRNA level was markedly inhibited by pre-treatment of CHX at the time points of 0.5 and 1 h after drug treatment whereas CHX treatment caused an enhancement of PMA-induced _fra-1_ mRNA level at 4 h. In addition, the treatment of CHX alone showed differential effects on basal expression of proto-oncogenes. CHX treatment alone increased the c- _fos_ and c- _jun_ mRNA levels at all time points whereas _fra-1_ mRNA level was increased only at 4, but not 0.5 and 1 h after CHX treatment. Although the explanations for these findings are not clearly known, at least four proposed contributory mechanisms can be speculated in in vitro experiments. First, the labile proto-oncogene transcripts, such as c- _fos_ and c- _jun_ mRNAs, become much more stable in the presence of protein synthesis inhibitor w6,9 .x Second, the translational arrest may cause the inability of auto-repression of proto-oncogene transcription by reducing its protein product level w7 .x Third, the protein synthesis inhibitor may block the synthesis labile repressors which attenuate the proto-oncogene transcription w44 .x Finally, a certain protein synthesis inhibitor, such as CHX, may act as a potent intracellular signaling agonist w15,28 .x

Indeed, previous reports have suggested that phosphorylation of CREBP is a major regulatory element for the induction of c- _fos_ mRNA and its translation w16,34 .x Therefore, phospho-CREBP may be involved in the regulation of proENK gene expression indirectly through the induction of c- _fos_ gene expression. The DNA sequence analyses of the proENK gene from human w11x and rat w20,21,37x revealed three homologous functional domains; ENKCRE-1, ENKCRE-2, and AP-2. In addition, a series of transfer transfection assays of the human proENK promoter-CAT activity showed that ENKCRE-2 element could confer responsiveness to phorbol esters and activation of cAMP w11 .x In electrophoretic mobility shift assay, both ENKCRE-2 and AP-1 DNA binding activities were elevated at 0.5 h and these increases were peaked at 1–4 h after PMA treatment ŽFig. 5A.. In addition, the increases of ENKCRE-2 and AP-1 DNA binding activities induced by PMA were effectively inhibited by the pre-treatment with CHX, suggesting that the newly synthesized proteins may be responsible for increasing ENKCRE-2 and AP-1 DNA binding activities. Previous work has shown that ENKCRE-2 functions as an 

AP-1 or CRE w12 .x Therefore, either CREBP or protooncoprotein, such as Fos and Jun proteins can recognize ENKCRE-2 site, leading to activation of proENK transcription. Additionally, the cross-competitions between AP-1 and ENKCRE-2 showed that the AP-1 or ENKCRE-2 DNA binding activities were also diminished by unlabeled cross-competitor ŽFig. 5B.. These findings suggest that AP-1 DNA binding proteins, such as Fos and Jun family proteins, actively participate in ENKCRE-2 DNA binding activity. 

Although we found, in the present study, that PMA-induced proENK mRNA level was well correlated with AP-1 and ENKCRE-2 DNA binding activities which may be activated by AP-1 DNA binding proteins such as c-Fos, Fra-1, and c-Jun proteins, possible involvement of other untested proto-oncoproteins in the ENKCRE-2 and AP-1 DNA binding activities cannot be excluded. Additionally, post-translational modification such as phosphorylation of proto-oncoproteins may alter the transcriptional activity of proENK gene w5,33,35 .x Furthermore, the human proENK promoter-CAT activity showed that AP-2 DNA domain in proENK promoterrenhancer region can confer responsiveness to phorbol esters. Although many reports have suggested that AP-1 and ENKCRE-2 domains are major candidates for the regulation of proENK mRNA expression, the studies on exact nature of interaction between these transcriptional factors and proENK genes should be further determined in the future study.
