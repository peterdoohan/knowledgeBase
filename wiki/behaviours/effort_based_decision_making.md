## Key evidence

- Bidirectional DREADD manipulation of ACC pyramidal neurons (CaMKII promoter) in rats impairs effort-based choice whether population activity is increased or decreased, and population-level calcium activity (not single cells) is the best predictor of choice — consistent with a role for E/I balance in value computation. ([Izquierdo 2024](../../raw/summaries/izquierdo2024_prefrontal_cortex_homology.md))

- Optogenetic silencing of ACC excitatory neurons during action selection acutely impairs mice's preference to exert greater effort for a larger reward in a barrier T-maze. stGtACR2 silencing reduced high-reward arm choices from ~75% (light OFF) to ~45% (light ON); mCherry controls showed no effect. ([Kashay 2023](../../raw/summaries/kashay2023_ACC_effort_decision.md))

- ACC is required specifically during action selection and trial preparation, not during rest. Silencing during ITI alone had no effect; silencing during start box + ITI reduced high-reward choices; silencing during choice period produced strongest disruption. ([Kashay 2023](../../raw/summaries/kashay2023_ACC_effort_decision.md))

- ACC silencing disrupts the microstructure of goal-directed movement by introducing micropauses in the approach trajectory. DeepLabCut tracking revealed frequent micropauses (>2 frames without progress) in approach zone during high-effort choices when ACC was silenced. ([Kashay 2023](../../raw/summaries/kashay2023_ACC_effort_decision.md))

- ACC is required specifically for goal-directed effort-based decision making, not for general mobility or non-goal-directed effort. Silencing had no effect on open field mean velocity or tail suspension test effortful behavior; effects specific to barrier T-maze context. ([Kashay 2023](../../raw/summaries/kashay2023_ACC_effort_decision.md))
