## Key evidence

- Adult mice can spontaneously re-express coordinated hindlimb locomotion within 14 days of complete thoracic spinal cord transection without pharmacological treatment, demonstrating intrinsic spinal cord capacity for locomotor recovery ([Leblond2003](../../raw/summaries/leblond2003_locomotion_spinal.md)).

- Intralimb coordination recovers before interlimb alternation following spinal cord injury, suggesting hierarchical organization of spinal locomotor networks with limb-specific rhythm generators becoming functional before coupling circuits ([Leblond2003](../../raw/summaries/leblond2003_locomotion_spinal.md)).

- Step cycle duration in spinal mice is significantly longer than intact mice (700±64 ms vs 500±94 ms, p<0.0005) due to prolonged stance rather than swing phase, indicating differential recovery of locomotor parameters ([Leblond2003](../../raw/summaries/leblond2003_locomotion_spinal.md)).
