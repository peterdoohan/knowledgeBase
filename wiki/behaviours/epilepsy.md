# Epilepsy

## Current understanding

Epilepsy involves dysregulated neuronal excitability and synchronization, with seizure susceptibility arising from imbalances in excitatory and inhibitory neurotransmission. Homeostatic mechanisms, glial network function, and multiple molecular targets including GABAA receptors, ion channels, and endocannabinoid system components contribute to seizure threshold regulation.

## Key evidence

- Cannabigerolic acid (CBGA) shows anticonvulsant effects in Scn1a+/- mice (Dravet syndrome model) at 30 mg/kg, more potent than CBD (effective at 100 mg/kg) ([Anderson 2021](../../raw/summaries/anderson2021_cannabigerolic_seizures.md))

- CBGA exhibits proconvulsant effects at high doses (2500 mg/kg chow increases spontaneous generalized tonic-clonic seizure frequency), indicating a narrow therapeutic window ([Anderson 2021](../../raw/summaries/anderson2021_cannabigerolic_seizures.md))

- CBGA is a positive allosteric modulator (PAM) of GABAA receptors (EC50 910 nM, Emax 271%) but causes receptor desensitization with prolonged exposure (180 s preincubation), providing a mechanism for both anticonvulsant and proconvulsant effects ([Anderson 2021](../../raw/summaries/anderson2021_cannabigerolic_seizures.md))

- CBGA inhibits GPR55 (IC50 ~5 μM) as a non-competitive antagonist and TRPV1 (IC50 22 μM) but shows no activity at CB1 or CB2 receptors ([Anderson 2021](../../raw/summaries/anderson2021_cannabigerolic_seizures.md))

- CBGA combined with sub-threshold clobazam (1 mg/kg) shows significantly enhanced anticonvulsant effects against hyperthermia-induced seizures, and the combination reduces spontaneous seizure frequency compared to controls ([Anderson 2021](../../raw/summaries/anderson2021_cannabigerolic_seizures.md))

- Combined CBD and clobazam shows enhanced anticonvulsant efficacy through both pharmacokinetic interaction (CBD inhibits CYP3A4/CYP2C19, increasing clobazam levels) and pharmacodynamic interaction (cooperative GABAA receptor potentiation) ([Anderson 2019](../../raw/summaries/anderson2019_cannabidiol_clobazam_seizure.md))

- CBD potently inhibits CYP3A4-mediated clobazam metabolism (Ki = 139 nmol/L) and less potently inhibits CYP2C19-mediated N-desmethylclobazam metabolism (Ki = 2.6 μmol/L) ([Anderson 2019](../../raw/summaries/anderson2019_cannabidiol_clobazam_seizure.md))

- A sub-anticonvulsant dose of CBD (12 mg/kg) that elevates clobazam plasma levels does NOT augment anticonvulsant efficacy, demonstrating that pharmacokinetic interaction alone is insufficient for enhanced efficacy ([Anderson 2019](../../raw/summaries/anderson2019_cannabidiol_clobazam_seizure.md))

- CBD and clobazam co-applied to GABAA receptors (α1β2γ2) produce additive potentiation of GABA-evoked currents (289% vs 204% for clobazam alone, 82% for CBD alone), acting via distinct binding sites ([Anderson 2019](../../raw/summaries/anderson2019_cannabidiol_clobazam_seizure.md))

- CBD + clobazam combination significantly improves survival in Scn1a+/- mice compared to either monotherapy (p = 0.0033), a clinically important finding for SUDEP (sudden unexpected death in epilepsy) risk reduction ([Anderson 2019](../../raw/summaries/anderson2019_cannabidiol_clobazam_seizure.md))

- Astroglial metabolic networks can sustain epileptiform activity during glucose deprivation—glucose trafficking through astrocytic networks maintains 31% of bursts versus almost total abolition without glucose ([Rouach 2008](../../raw/summaries/rouach2008_astrocyte_metabolism.md))

## History of ideas

The understanding of epilepsy mechanisms has evolved from purely neuronal-centric models to recognition of glial contributions and network-level homeostatic dysregulation. The discovery that cannabinoids have anticonvulsant properties has led to regulatory approval of CBD for Dravet syndrome, and recent work has begun characterizing the contributions of other phytocannabinoids like CBGA. The interaction between cannabinoids and conventional antiepileptic drugs (like clobazam) involves both pharmacokinetic and pharmacodynamic mechanisms, highlighting the complexity of polypharmacy in epilepsy treatment.

## Open questions

- The metabolic pathways of CBGA are unknown; proconvulsant or anticonvulsant effects could be mediated by metabolites rather than CBGA itself
- Brain penetration of CBGA is low (~2% brain:plasma ratio), limiting direct CNS bioavailability
- Whether the therapeutic window of CBGA can be optimized through analogue development or formulation
- The specific molecular target(s) mediating anticonvulsant and proconvulsant effects in vivo remain unresolved
- How astroglial metabolic network dysfunction contributes to seizure susceptibility in different epilepsy types
