# Novelty Seeking

Novelty seeking is a fundamental motivated behavior that drives organisms to explore and acquire information about novel stimuli, objects, conspecifics, or environments. It represents a distinct behavioral mode from routine or familiar-environment behavior.

## Key evidence

### Deep vs. shallow investigation states

- Investigatory behavior can be decomposed into latent states using Hidden Markov Models (HMM): idle, shallow investigation, and deep investigation. In object investigation, shallow = sniff-without-bite, while deep = sniff-followed-by-bite. In social investigation, shallow = approach-investigation-without-grab, while deep = approach-with-grab. The only action whose transition probability differs between familiar and novel objects is sniff-to-bite, suggesting deep investigation is the specific behavioral signature of novelty-seeking ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

### Neural circuit for novelty-seeking

- A cortico-subcortical circuit gates novelty-seeking behavior: prelimbic cortex (PL) → zona incerta medial (ZIm) → periaqueductal gray (PAG). This circuit specifically controls the transition from shallow to deep investigation of novel stimuli ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

- ZIm^TAC1 (tachykinin 1-expressing) GABAergic neurons are the specific cell type that drives novelty-seeking. Photo-activation of these neurons increases novel object investigation duration by ~4-fold and shifts behavior toward deep investigation states. This effect is specific to TAC1 neurons—activation of ZIm^PV or ZIm^SST neurons does not produce this effect ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

### Selectivity for novelty

- Mice display dramatically more deep investigation of novel versus familiar objects (deep vs. shallow preference index: novel 0.51 ± 0.12 vs. familiar -0.45 ± 0.17 rad, p < 10⁻⁴). The neural circuit specifically modulates novelty-seeking rather than general investigatory behavior ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

### Dissociation from locomotion and approach

- The novelty-seeking circuit specifically modulates the depth or commitment of investigation rather than general motivational arousal. Inhibiting ZIm^GAD2 does not reduce open-field travel distance (locomotion preserved), and approach frequency is preserved—only deep engagement with objects is affected ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

### Rewarding nature of novelty-seeking

- ZIm^GAD2 activation is intrinsically rewarding or motivating. Mice self-stimulate ZIm^GAD2 neurons (106 ± 20 returns to opto-port vs. 673 ± 179 to non-opto, p < 0.05), and activation increases arousal (pupil dilation, whisker activity). This links the novelty-seeking circuit to affective value of curiosity ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

## Open questions

- How does the circuit discriminate novel from familiar stimuli at the sensory input level? The mechanism by which ZIm^TAC1 neurons integrate sensory novelty signals is not resolved ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

- What is the relationship between ZIm-driven curiosity and dopaminergic reward signals? This interaction was not investigated ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

- Does this circuit generalize to other species or to human curiosity circuits? This remains unaddressed ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).
