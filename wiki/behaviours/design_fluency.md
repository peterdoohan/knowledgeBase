## Key evidence

- Right frontal lobe lesions impair design fluency — the ability to invent novel nonsense drawings — while left temporal lobe lesions impair verbal fluency, dissociating the contributions of these regions to generative output. ([Jones-Gotman 1977](../../raw/summaries/jonesgotman1977_design_fluency_frontal.md))

- Right fronto-central lesions selectively impair design fluency (both free and fixed conditions) while word fluency is relatively preserved; left central lesions show the opposite pattern with verbal fluency impairment and relatively spared design fluency. ([Jones-Gotman 1977](../../raw/summaries/jonesgotman1977_design_fluency_frontal.md))

- Left temporal lobe lesion patients produced zero nameable drawings in drawing tasks, versus right temporal patients showing a tendency to produce recognisable (non-novel) forms, suggesting impaired suppression of familiar forms in right temporal lesions. ([Jones-Gotman 1977](../../raw/summaries/jonesgotman1977_design_fluency_frontal.md))
