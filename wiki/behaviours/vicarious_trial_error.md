## Key evidence

- Vicarious trial and error (VTE) — pause-and-look behavior at maze choice points — reflects deliberative decision-making supported by hippocampal theta sequences serially representing future path options ([Redish 2016](../../raw/summaries/redish2016_vicarious_trial_error.md))
- VTE occurs during flexible decision-making (early learning, changing contingencies, difficult choices) and disappears with automation, matching deliberation-hypothesis predictions ([Redish 2016](../../raw/summaries/redish2016_vicarious_trial_error.md))
- Hippocampal place-cell sequences during VTE project far ahead of the rat and alternate serially between the two path options within individual theta cycles, consistent with a search-and-evaluate process ([Redish 2016](../../raw/summaries/redish2016_vicarious_trial_error.md))
- Three stages of hippocampal sequence behavior are described: (1) deliberation — sequences alternate between both goal options; (2) planning — sequences sweep only toward the chosen goal; (3) automation — sequences collapse to the animal's immediate location ([Redish 2016](../../raw/summaries/redish2016_vicarious_trial_error.md))
- Hippocampal lesions and cannabinoid agonists (CP55940) that disrupt theta sequences increase VTE; clonidine (which reduces noradrenaline) decreases VTE and limits hippocampal search to a single path ([Redish 2016](../../raw/summaries/redish2016_vicarious_trial_error.md))
- Ventral striatum provides pre-decision reward evaluation signals during VTE, while orbitofrontal cortex provides post-decision expectation signals, and dorsolateral striatum shows task-bracketing without VTE-related activity ([Redish 2016](../../raw/summaries/redish2016_vicarious_trial_error.md))
- Dorsolateral striatum does not show transient forward sweeps or covert reward signals during VTE; instead, it develops task-bracketing activity (start/end firing) as behavior automates — the procedural system signature ([Redish 2016](../../raw/summaries/redish2016_vicarious_trial_error.md))

## History of ideas

- In the 1930s, Muenzinger, Gentry and Tolman observed that rats at maze choice points sometimes pause and look back and forth, proposing that this behavior (VTE) reflected prospective imagination of future options.
- This hypothesis fell out of favor because no mechanistic account existed, and the relevant computational and recording tools were unavailable.
- The 2007 discovery that hippocampal place-cell firing during VTE generates alternating forward representations of each path option (Johnson & Redish) revived the hypothesis.

## Open questions

- How the search process selects which futures to construct (goal-cueing, priority signals)
- The mechanism by which deliberation terminates and action is selected
- Whether dorsal and ventral hippocampus encode unified or dissociable representations during VTE
- The specific role of the dorsomedial striatum in deliberation
