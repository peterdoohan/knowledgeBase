## Key evidence

- Lateral frontal cortex is organized into three functionally discontinuous zones — sensory-motor, contextual, and schematic control — rather than a continuous rostrocaudal abstraction gradient ([Badre 2018](../../raw/summaries/badre2018_frontal_cortex_hierarchical_control.md))
- Mid-DLPFC (areas 45/46), not RLPFC, is the apex of the frontal processing hierarchy based on connectional asymmetry and effective connectivity ([Badre 2018](../../raw/summaries/badre2018_frontal_cortex_hierarchical_control.md))
- RLPFC functions as a domain-specific input region for schematic control, transmitting internally generated schema-based knowledge from vmPFC-hippocampal circuits into the contextual control hierarchy ([Badre 2018](../../raw/summaries/badre2018_frontal_cortex_hierarchical_control.md))
- Working memory gating via corticostriatal circuits is a core mechanism of hierarchical control, modulating rostrocaudal activation patterns beyond abstraction level per se ([Badre 2018](../../raw/summaries/badre2018_frontal_cortex_hierarchical_control.md))
- Theta-gamma phase-amplitude coupling between PFC and premotor/motor cortex increases with hierarchical control demands, with PFC theta phase preferentially predicting downstream gamma modulation ([Badre 2018](../../raw/summaries/badre2018_frontal_cortex_hierarchical_control.md))
- RLPFC activity ramps across serial positions within task sequences, with single-pulse TMS causing increasing errors at later sequence positions ([Badre 2019](../../raw/summaries/badre2019_hierarchical_cognitive_control_lobes.md))
- The three lateral frontal zones correspond to distributed frontoparietal networks that extend into parietal cortex with the same network boundaries ([Badre 2019](../../raw/summaries/badre2019_hierarchical_cognitive_control_lobes.md))
