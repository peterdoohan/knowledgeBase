## Key evidence

- The standard stay-probability analysis on the reduced two-step task can misidentify pure model-free agents as model-based due to correlations between trial-start action values and subsequent trial events. The mechanism: action values correlate with whether the agent chose the 'correct' action, which in turn correlates with which combination of transition and outcome will be observed ([Akam 2015](../../raw/summaries/akam2015_two_step_task_simple_plans.md)).

- Before error choices in CA1, goal-directed firing rates are significantly reduced (z = 5.61, p = 1.97 × 10⁻⁸) and the fantail is rotated away from the goal, even before the animal knows which platforms will be offered, suggesting the firing predicts choice rather than merely accompanying movement ([Ormond 2022](../../raw/summaries/ormond2022_goal_oriented_vector_fields.md))

- Trial-type specificity in RSC increases from chance early in learning to 21% of neurons at asymptote; linear classifier on population activity reaches perfect accuracy at asymptote and correlates with within-session performance (F(3,132) = 24.38, p < 0.001) ([Miller 2019](../../raw/summaries/miller2019_retrosplenial_spatial_learning.md))

- Splitter cells in CA1, CA3, mEC, and SUB encode current route identity rather than goal identity per se; only ~0.5% of place cells show goal-specific (route-independent) firing ([Nyberg 2022](../../raw/summaries/nyberg2022_spatial_goal_coding.md))

---

- OFC goal coding is viewpoint-invariant and represents the chosen (not correct) goal ([Basu2021](../../raw/summaries/basu2021_ofc_navigation_goals.md))

- Ventral hippocampal inputs are necessary for OFC's representation of target direction during decision-making ([Basu2021](../../raw/summaries/basu2023_goal_pointer_cognitive_map_ofc.md))
