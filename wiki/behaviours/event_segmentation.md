## Key evidence

- Event representations emerge from temporal community structure rather than from predictive uncertainty or surprise at event boundaries. Participants consistently segmented sequences at community boundaries even when transition probabilities were uniform and local statistics controlled (Exps 1-2; t(29)=2.27, P<0.05; t(9)=2.30, P<0.05), demonstrating that parsing occurs without prediction error signals. ([Schapiro 2013](../../raw/summaries/schapiro2013_event_representation_memory.md))

- Medial prefrontal cortex tracks event structure through sustained activity during within-community periods and transient disengagement at boundaries. mPFC showed sustained activity during within-community periods and transient disengagement at community boundaries (P<0.05 corrected), consistent with tracking event structure rather than surprise. ([Schapiro 2013](../../raw/summaries/schapiro2013_event_representation_memory.md))

- Left inferior frontal gyrus and insula represent community membership through repetition enhancement effects. Bilateral IFG and insula showed progressively stronger responses as participants viewed more items from the same community (P<0.05 corrected), suggesting neural populations representing community membership through accumulated activation. ([Schapiro 2013](../../raw/summaries/schapiro2013_event_representation_memory.md))

- Multivoxel patterns in left IFG, insula, ATL, and STG represent items within the same community as more similar than items from different communities. MVPA in left IFG, insula, anterior temporal lobe, and superior temporal gyrus showed items within the same community were represented as more similar than items from different communities (P<0.05 corrected), demonstrating community-structured representational geometry. ([Schapiro 2013](../../raw/summaries/schapiro2013_event_representation_memory.md))

- A simple predictive learning model develops community-structured internal representations through error-driven learning. A three-layer feedforward neural network trained to predict next items developed clustered hidden-layer representations mirroring graph communities (t(19)=140.84, P<0.0001), with representational dynamics that could support parsing behavior. ([Schapiro 2013](../../raw/summaries/schapiro2013_event_representation_memory.md))
