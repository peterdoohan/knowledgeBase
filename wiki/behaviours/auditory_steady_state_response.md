## Key evidence

- Increased self-inhibition of superficial pyramidal cells—reflecting reduced synaptic gain—is the consistent circuit abnormality across resting-state EEG, MMN, 40-Hz ASSR, and rsfMRI in schizophrenia. ([Adams 2021](../../raw/summaries/adams2021_pyramidal_synaptic_gain.md))
- A single computational mechanism—reduced pyramidal synaptic gain—can explain diverse electrophysiological biomarkers in schizophrenia including reduced MMN, reduced 40-Hz ASSR, and resting-state EEG spectral abnormalities. ([Adams 2021](../../raw/summaries/adams2021_pyramidal_synaptic_gain.md))
