## Key evidence

- A subset of PFC neurons is selectively tuned to the content of hippocampal replay rather than the animal's actual location, with 18.5% of PFC neurons significantly differentially modulated by replay of different maze arms (p=2.2e-9) ([BernersLee2021](../../raw/summaries/bernerslee2021_prefrontal_cortex_hippocampal.md)).

- PFC neurons are preferentially driven by non-local hippocampal representations during both replay and behavior, with stronger responses to hippocampal spikes from late (non-local) than early (local) theta phases (p=4.9e-5) ([BernersLee2021](../../raw/summaries/bernerslee2021_prefrontal_cortex_hippocampal.md)).

- PFC activity locked to non-local theta epochs predicts upcoming choice while PFC activity locked to local epochs does not, demonstrating functional significance of non-local representations for decision-making ([BernersLee2021](../../raw/summaries/bernerslee2021_prefrontal_cortex_hippocampal.md)).

- Hippocampal non-local representations during theta sequences are SWR- and theta-locked, in contrast to mPFC non-local representations which are not temporally locked to hippocampal oscillations, suggesting distinct mechanisms ([denBakker2024](../../raw/summaries/denbakker2024_mpfc_spatial_swr.md)).
