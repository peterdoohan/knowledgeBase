## Key evidence

- Hippocampal theta sequences during vicarious trial and error project far ahead of the rat and alternate serially between path options, consistent with a search-and-evaluate process during deliberative decision-making ([Redish2016](../../raw/summaries/redish2016_vicarious_trial_error.md)).

- Three stages of hippocampal sequence behavior are described: deliberation (sequences alternate between both goal options), planning (sequences sweep only toward chosen goal), and automation (sequences collapse to animal's immediate location) ([Redish2016](../../raw/summaries/redish2016_vicarious_trial_error.md)).

- Hippocampal place cells fire phase-precessed within the theta LFP, generating compressed sequences representing paths forward of the rat; this phase relationship is disrupted by cannabinoid agonists that increase VTE ([Redish2016](../../raw/summaries/redish2016_vicarious_trial_error.md)).

- PFC neurons are preferentially driven by non-local hippocampal representations during late theta phases, with PFC activity locked to non-local theta epochs predicting upcoming choice while local-locked activity does not ([BernersLee2021](../../raw/summaries/bernerslee2021_prefrontal_cortex_hippocampal.md)).
