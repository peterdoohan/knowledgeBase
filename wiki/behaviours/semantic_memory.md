## Key evidence

- Human medial temporal lobe contains concept cells that encode semantic concepts regardless of sensory modality or visual format, responding to specific people or objects across images, sketches, written and spoken names ([Kubska2021](../../raw/summaries/kubska2021_single_neuron_cognition.md)).

- Concept cells are not found in other species to date and reflect perceptual decisions and conscious recognition rather than low-level visual features, suggesting human-specific neural mechanisms for semantic representation ([Kubska2021](../../raw/summaries/kubska2021_single_neuron_cognition.md)).

- Human concept cells rapidly expand tuning to newly associated stimuli after brief exposure, providing cellular evidence for one-shot associative encoding in semantic memory ([Kubska2021](../../raw/summaries/kubska2021_single_neuron_cognition.md)).
