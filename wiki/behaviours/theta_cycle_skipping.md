## Key evidence

- Theta cycle skipping is more prevalent in SWR-unmodulated mPFC neurons (28.94%) than SWR-excited (22.72%) or SWR-inhibited (12.39%) neurons (p<0.001), suggesting distinct functional roles for theta-bursting neurons ([denBakker2024](../../raw/summaries/denbakker2024_mpfc_spatial_swr.md)).

- Theta cycle skipping in mPFC predicts upcoming choice, with SWR-unmodulated neurons showing stronger predictive coding for spatial decisions ([denBakker2024](../../raw/summaries/denbakker2024_mpfc_spatial_swr.md)).

- Theta cycle skipping may reflect burst firing patterns at theta frequency that enable selective communication between brain regions during specific theta phases, facilitating coordination of hippocampal-prefrontal interactions ([denBakker2024](../../raw/summaries/denbakker2024_mpfc_spatial_swr.md)).
