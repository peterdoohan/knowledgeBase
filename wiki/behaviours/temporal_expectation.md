# Temporal Expectation

Temporal expectation refers to the ability to predict when events will occur in time. This cognitive function allows organisms to optimize perception and action by allocating attention and motor preparation resources to expected moments of stimulus onset.

## Key evidence

- CTOA (temporal expectation) is encoded in two distinct low-dimensional components in FEF with different neural profiles — one linear and one inverted-U shaped; dPCA captured CTOA variance in two orthogonal components: one with linear activation profile (R² = 0.98 at trial level) correlating with reaction time, and one with inverted-U profile (R² = 0.97) correlating with attentional focus ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- The CTOA-encoding components are non-orthogonal to both reaction time and attentional focus components, creating representational overlap that predicts behavioral interactions; dPCA angular analysis showed CTOA components were significantly non-orthogonal to both RT and TA components, with activation of first CTOA dPC correlating with RT across sessions (R² = 0.79) and second CTOA dPC correlating with TA (R² = 0.49) ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- Non-orthogonal coding for temporal expectation in FEF is a systematic strategy for maximizing encoding capacity under dimensionality constraints, with predictable behavioral costs; the geometric arrangement produces behavioral correlations between temporal expectation, attention, and reaction time, establishing a formal link between neural geometry and overt behavior ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))
