# Sharp-wave ripple-associated hippocampal replay

Sharp-wave ripple (SWR)-associated replay is the best-established form of hippocampal sequence reactivation: temporally compressed place-cell sequences expressed during CA3-CA1 sharp-wave ripples, especially during awake immobility and non-REM sleep. The strongest claims now supported are narrower than older “replay explains memory” narratives: SWR events can rapidly encode recent experience, can represent spatial trajectories and relational structure rather than literal episode copies, and can causally support some forms of spatial memory when ripple timing or content is manipulated. Harder claims—that replay directly determines upcoming choices on each trial, or that all SWRs serve the same function—remain mixed and task-dependent.

## Current view

SWR-associated replay is usually treated as a population event generated by CA3-driven sharp waves with locally paced CA1 ripples, then read out across distributed targets [[raw/summaries/buzsaki2015_hippocampal_sharp_wave_ripple|Buzsáki 2015]]. This matters because replay is not just a decoded pattern; it is embedded in a specific hippocampal network state.

The content of replay is now viewed as structured and selective, not a faithful tape of recent behavior. Replay can appear after very little experience, can run forward or reverse, can depict remote or novel trajectories, and often looks more like sampling from a learned spatial model than verbatim recapitulation [[raw/summaries/foster2017_replay_memory_consolidation|Foster 2017]] [[raw/summaries/mckenzie2022_hippocampal_replay|McKenzie 2022]].

Functionally, the most defensible synthesis is that SWR replay supports memory stabilization and model updating, with a stronger role during learning, novelty, and rule change than during asymptotic performance [[raw/summaries/singer2013_hippocampal_swr_decisions|Singer et al. 2013]] [[raw/summaries/fernandez2019_ripples_memory_consolidation|Fernandez-Ruiz et al. 2019]] [[raw/summaries/denbakker2023_sharp_wave_prefrontal_rule|Den Bakker et al. 2023]]. Claims about direct online planning are plausible and in some tasks well supported, but not universal [[raw/summaries/igata2021_prioritized_experience_replays|Igata et al. 2021]] [[raw/summaries/gillespie2021_replay_past_experiences|Gillespie et al. 2021]].

A useful update is that not all ripples are equivalent. Ripple duration, recruited cell ensemble, and downstream coordination all matter; long, content-rich SWRs are more behaviorally relevant than short or randomly induced events [[raw/summaries/fernandez2019_ripples_memory_consolidation|Fernandez-Ruiz et al. 2019]].

## Strongest evidence

- **Causal memory benefit from prolonging ongoing ripples.** In a spatial memory task, prolonging ongoing SWRs improved memory-dependent outbound performance, while randomly induced ripples of similar duration did not. The benefit tracked recruitment of additional spatially selective cells representing more of the planned route, arguing that ripple **content and state dependence**, not mere oscillation occurrence, matter [[raw/summaries/fernandez2019_ripples_memory_consolidation|Fernandez-Ruiz et al. 2019]].

- **Selective disruption of replay causes selective memory loss.** Assembly-specific disruption of hippocampal replay during sleep impaired recall of the disrupted memories while sparing undisrupted ones, which is among the cleanest arguments that replay content itself is functionally relevant [[raw/summaries/gridchyn2020_replay_selective_memory|Gridchyn et al. 2020]].

- **Replay content can be biased experimentally.** Cueing sleeping animals with task-associated sounds biases replay toward the cued memory, providing direct leverage over replay content during consolidation rather than just global arousal or state changes [[raw/summaries/bendor2012_biasing_hippocampal_replay_sleep|Bendor et al. 2012]].

- **SWR-linked activity predicts successful decisions during learning.** On a spatial alternation task, stronger SWR coactivation predicted correct versus incorrect trials during early learning, with the effect disappearing after overtraining. This supports a role in learning-sensitive decision support, though it is correlational and only modestly predictive [[raw/summaries/singer2013_hippocampal_swr_decisions|Singer et al. 2013]].

- **Replay can emerge after one experience.** Significant CA1 replay appears within an hour after a single traversal of a novel track, then becomes longer and spatially finer with additional experience. This strongly constrains models that require repeated rehearsal before replay can form [[raw/summaries/mckenzie2022_hippocampal_replay|McKenzie 2022]].

- **Downstream use of SWR information is causal in rule updating.** Inhibiting medial prefrontal cortex specifically during hippocampal SWRs impairs spatial rule switching, whereas delayed inhibition does not. This is not a content-decoding result, but it is strong evidence that SWR-timed hippocampal-cortical communication is behaviorally consequential [[raw/summaries/denbakker2023_sharp_wave_prefrontal_rule|Den Bakker et al. 2023]].

## Landmark papers

- [[raw/summaries/buzsaki2015_hippocampal_sharp_wave_ripple|Buzsáki 2015]]: established the modern physiological framework for SPW-R generation and positioned SWRs as a core substrate for replay, consolidation, and planning-related functions.

- [[raw/summaries/foster2017_replay_memory_consolidation|Foster 2017]]: reframed replay from “compressed memory trace” to selective sampling from an internal spatial model, emphasizing awake replay, novelty, remote content, and novel trajectories.

- [[raw/summaries/singer2013_hippocampal_swr_decisions|Singer et al. 2013]]: a key empirical link between SWR reactivation strength and trial-by-trial performance during initial learning.

- [[raw/summaries/bendor2012_biasing_hippocampal_replay_sleep|Bendor et al. 2012]]: showed that replay content during sleep can be externally biased, helping move the field beyond purely observational work.

- [[raw/summaries/fernandez2019_ripples_memory_consolidation|Fernandez-Ruiz et al. 2019]]: one of the strongest causal demonstrations that the duration and recruited content of ongoing SWRs influence spatial memory.

- [[raw/summaries/gridchyn2020_replay_selective_memory|Gridchyn et al. 2020]]: sharpened the causal claim further by linking disruption of specific replay assemblies to selective memory deficits.

## Boundaries and tensions

The biggest unresolved tension is **planning versus memory maintenance**. Some work supports prioritized, future-efficient replay and links it to flexible navigation or learning [[raw/summaries/igata2021_prioritized_experience_replays|Igata et al. 2021]] [[raw/summaries/epsztein2022_mental_replays_navigation|Epsztein et al. 2022]]. But other work finds awake replay largely decoupled from the animal’s next choice, instead overrepresenting previously rewarded or nonrecent locations [[raw/summaries/gillespie2021_replay_past_experiences|Gillespie et al. 2021]]. A simple “replay = imminent plan” account does not survive this literature.

A second tension is **consolidation versus online computation**. Sleep replay manipulations strongly support a consolidation role [[raw/summaries/bendor2012_biasing_hippocampal_replay_sleep|Bendor et al. 2012]] [[raw/summaries/gridchyn2020_replay_selective_memory|Gridchyn et al. 2020]], while awake SWRs also track early learning, strategy updating, and possibly prospective evaluation [[raw/summaries/singer2013_hippocampal_swr_decisions|Singer et al. 2013]] [[raw/summaries/denbakker2023_sharp_wave_prefrontal_rule|Den Bakker et al. 2023]]. The same event class may support different computations across states, but the division is not cleanly mapped.

There is also a **heterogeneity problem**. Ripple duration, sequence direction, represented distance, and ensemble composition all vary, and these differences appear functionally meaningful [[raw/summaries/fernandez2019_ripples_memory_consolidation|Fernandez-Ruiz et al. 2019]] [[raw/summaries/foster2017_replay_memory_consolidation|Foster 2017]]. Treating all SWRs as interchangeable likely obscures the relevant biology.

A fourth tension concerns **what exactly is replaying**. Spatial studies dominate, but SWR-associated reactivation can also reflect inferred relationships that were never directly experienced together [[raw/summaries/barron2020_neuronal_computation_inferential|Barron et al. 2020]]. That broadens the construct beyond simple place-sequence reinstatement, but also makes definitions less stable.

Finally, some headline ideas remain method-sensitive. Claims about preplay and about how much structure is genuinely novel versus preconfigured depend heavily on decoding choices and null models [[raw/summaries/buzsaki2015_hippocampal_sharp_wave_ripple|Buzsáki 2015]] [[raw/summaries/mckenzie2022_hippocampal_replay|McKenzie 2022]].

## Open questions

- What is causally important in an SWR event: the ripple itself, the specific decoded sequence, the event duration, or its coordination with downstream regions?

- When does awake SWR replay affect **immediate choice**, versus slower learning, credit assignment, or memory stabilization across trials?

- How are replay contents selected by novelty, reward, task demands, and latent structure?

- What is the division of labor across CA3, CA1, entorhinal cortex, and prefrontal cortex in generating, shaping, and reading out replay?

- Are forward and reverse replay mechanistically distinct processes, or different operating modes of the same circuit dynamics?

- How general is SWR replay outside canonical spatial navigation—to inference, schema updating, and nonspatial relational memory—without stretching the term past usefulness?

- Which aspects of reported “novel” or “prospective” replay reflect genuine model-based construction, versus biases introduced by limited sampling and decoder assumptions?
