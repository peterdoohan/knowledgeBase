# Prelimbic Cortex

The prelimbic cortex (PL) is a subregion of the medial prefrontal cortex in rodents, homologous to dorsal anterior cingulate cortex in primates. It is implicated in executive function, working memory, and the regulation of goal-directed behavior.

## Key evidence

### Role in investigatory behavior

- Prelimbic cortex provides excitatory input to zona incerta medial (ZIm) that is required for deep investigation of novel stimuli. Chemogenetic silencing of PL→ZIm terminals abolishes deep investigation, reducing investigation duration from ~73s to ~2s (p < 10⁻⁵). This demonstrates that prefrontal drive to ZIm is necessary for committed investigatory behavior ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

- The PL→ZIm projection is required for investigation of both objects and conspecifics, suggesting a general role in driving investigatory motivation across stimulus categories ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

### Computational role

- The PL signal to ZIm is hypothesized to convey non-specific motivational drive that is gated and thresholded by ZIm based on sensory context. This represents a gain-modulation mechanism where cortical excitation is multiplied by subcortical sensory context before triggering behavioral output. However, it remains unclear whether the PL signal conveys novelty-specific information or general motivational arousal ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

### Historical context

- Prior to the identification of its role in investigatory behavior via the ZI circuit, PL was known to encode social and spatial information and project to various subcortical structures. Its connections to hypothalamus and brainstem were established in the context of stress responses and defensive behaviors, but the PL→ZIm pathway for appetitive investigation was previously unknown ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

### Cell-type-specific circuit organization

- Prelimbic cortex is the primary focus for cell-type-specific circuit mapping in mPFC; it receives long-range inputs that are laminar- and cell-type-specific: BLA preferentially targets L2 cortico-amygdalar neurons; mediodorsal thalamus strongly drives L3 IT cells; ventromedial thalamus enriches inputs at distal apical dendrites of L5 PT cells; and ventral hippocampus preferentially targets L5 IT cells ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- BLA and vHPC show graded innervation across ACC, PL, and IL, with distinct laminar and cell-type targeting in each subregion, indicating wiring rules differ along the dorsoventral axis of mPFC ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- In local PL excitatory circuits, IT cells strongly innervate PT cells (but not vice versa), imposing a directional flow that ensures local computations are funnelled into output pathways ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- PL interneurons show biased connectivity preferentially targeting L5 PT cells over IT cells, with PV+, SOM+, CCK+, and NDNF+ interneurons all exhibiting this PT-targeting bias, meaning inhibition is organized to selectively gate output pathways rather than globally suppress activity ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- Prelimbic cortex lesions abolish contingency sensitivity and render behavior habit-like, with PL-lesioned rats failing to discriminate actions under non-contingent schedules and showing no reward-devaluation effects, though they can still learn reward-based discriminations ([Balleine 1998](../../raw/summaries/balleine1998_goal_directed_instrumental_action.md))

- Prelimbic cortex is necessary for goal-directed action but not for basic reward learning or S-R habit formation, establishing a double dissociation with insular cortex (which is required for incentive learning but not contingency sensitivity) ([Balleine 1998](../../raw/summaries/balleine1998_goal_directed_instrumental_action.md))

## Open questions

- Does PL convey novelty-specific information to ZIm, or does it provide a non-specific motivational signal that is gated by novelty detectors elsewhere? Distinguishing these possibilities requires recording PL activity during investigation of novel vs. familiar stimuli with and without ZIm manipulation ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

- What is the temporal dynamics of PL→ZIm signaling? Does PL provide a sustained drive during deep investigation, or phasic bursts at state transitions? High-temporal-resolution recordings are needed ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

- Are there other PL projection targets that work in parallel with ZIm to control investigation, or is ZIm the primary subcortical gateway? ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md))
