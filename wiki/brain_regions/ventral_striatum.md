## Key evidence

- Ventral striatum encodes prediction errors in structure-specific multivoxel patterns, suggesting structure-conditioned credit assignment. Multivoxel patterns of prediction error signals differed by relational structure in ventral striatum (p=0.034), with strong univariate PE signals (t(27)=10.24) ([Baram 2021](../../raw/summaries/baram2021_entorhinal_ventromedial_rl.md))

- Ventral striatum exhibits a standard hemodynamic response function for prediction error signals peaking at approximately 6 seconds post-outcome, in contrast to vmPFC's delayed peak at ~8.5 seconds ([Baram 2021](../../raw/summaries/baram2021_entorhinal_ventromedial_rl.md))

- Nucleus accumbens core dopamine release tracks a retrospective causal associative signal (ANCCR) rather than temporal difference reward prediction errors (TDRL RPE), challenging the dominant theory of dopamine's role in reward learning ([Jeong 2022](../../raw/summaries/jeong2022_mesolimbic_dopamine_causal.md)).

- In unpredicted reward paradigms, NAcc dopamine responses increase across sessions to a high positive asymptote and correlate positively with preceding inter-reward intervals, consistent with ANCCR predictions but opposite to TDRL RPE predictions ([Jeong 2022](../../raw/summaries/jeong2022_mesolimbic_dopamine_causal.md)).

- During cue-reward learning, dopaminergic cue responses in NAcc emerge and reach peak before behavioral learning (anticipatory licking), consistent with ANCCR's prediction that dopaminergic learning precedes behavioral learning, inconsistent with TDRL's tight coupling ([Jeong 2022](../../raw/summaries/jeong2022_mesolimbic_dopamine_causal.md)).

- After extinction, NAcc dopamine cue responses remain significantly positive long after behavioral extinction, consistent with ANCCR's maintenance of retrospective associations, inconsistent with TDRL which predicts cue response decay to zero with behavior ([Jeong 2022](../../raw/summaries/jeong2022_mesolimbic_dopamine_causal.md)).

- In sequential conditioning, dopamine responses to second-order and first-order cues increase together early in learning before diverging, consistent with ANCCR, inconsistent with TDRL's ordered backpropagation prediction ([Jeong 2022](../../raw/summaries/jeong2022_mesolimbic_dopamine_causal.md)).
