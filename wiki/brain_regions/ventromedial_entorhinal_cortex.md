## Key evidence

- The ventromedial band of the entorhinal cortex mediates defensive behavior but is not required for spatial memory; rats with ventromedial band lesions showed intact spatial memory retention (32.7% time in target zone vs. 35.5% for sham controls) ([Steffenach 2005](../../raw/summaries/steffenach2005_spatial_memory_entorhinal.md))

- The ventromedial band connects primarily to the ventral hippocampus, which is involved in emotion-related processing and defensive behaviors ([Steffenach 2005](../../raw/summaries/steffenach2005_spatial_memory_entorhinal.md))

- Ventromedial lesions affect defensive behavior as shown by increased entries into open arms on the elevated plus maze (group difference: t(8) = 2.6, p < 0.05) ([Steffenach 2005](../../raw/summaries/steffenach2005_spatial_memory_entorhinal.md))
