# Mediodorsal Thalamus

The mediodorsal thalamus (MD) is a higher-order thalamic nucleus that forms extensive reciprocal connections with the prefrontal cortex. It plays a critical role in working memory, attention, and cognitive control.

## Key evidence

### Connectivity with medial prefrontal cortex

- Mediodorsal thalamus provides prominent L1/L3 input to mPFC and strongly drives L3 intratelencephalic (IT) cells; this connectivity is key to its role in working memory and attention ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- MD drives parvalbumin+ (PV+) and somatostatin+ (SOM+) cells in L3 and VIP+ cells in L1b, showing specific recruitment of distinct interneuron populations ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- Many long-range inputs, including MD thalamic inputs, preferentially contact neurons that project back to the input region, providing a synaptic basis for strong reciprocal loops (mPFC↔thalamus) ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

## Open questions

- Whether findings from mouse PL generalise to other subregions, other rodents, or primates remains unresolved ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))
