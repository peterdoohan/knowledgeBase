## Key evidence

- The right entorhinal cortex encodes the relational structure of abstract reinforcement learning problems, generalizing across different sensory exemplars of the same structure. Representational similarity analysis showed this was the strongest whole-brain cluster (p=0.005 small-volume corrected), with representations for the same relational structure (different stimuli) being more similar than those for different structures ([Baram 2021](../../raw/summaries/baram2021_entorhinal_ventromedial_rl.md))

- The entorhinal cortex represents abstract task structure in a manner analogous to its representation of spatial structure during spatial remapping. EC representations preserved relational structure across different stimulus sets (same structure) but changed between different structures, mirroring how grid cells and place cells remap between environments while preserving spatial relationships ([Baram 2021](../../raw/summaries/baram2021_entorhinal_ventromedial_rl.md))

- The dorsolateral band of the entorhinal cortex is necessary for recall of spatial memory; rats with dorsolateral band lesions spent only 8.7% ± 2.9% of search time in platform zone vs. 38.0% ± 3.0% for sham controls, with 13 of 14 rats performing at chance level ([Steffenach 2005](../../raw/summaries/steffenach2005_spatial_memory_entorhinal.md))

- The ventromedial band of the entorhinal cortex mediates defensive behavior but is not required for spatial memory; rats with ventromedial band lesions showed intact spatial memory retention (32.7% time in target zone vs. 35.5% for sham controls) but made more entries into open arms on elevated plus maze ([Steffenach 2005](../../raw/summaries/steffenach2005_spatial_memory_entorhinal.md))

- The entorhinal cortex has a modular functional organization that mirrors the dorsal-ventral specialization of the hippocampus; dorsolateral band projects to dorsal hippocampus (spatial processing) and receives visuospatial input from postrhinal cortex, while ventromedial band projects to ventral hippocampus (emotion-related behaviors) ([Steffenach 2005](../../raw/summaries/steffenach2005_spatial_memory_entorhinal.md))

- The human entorhinal cortex implicitly encodes a map-like metric representation of discrete non-spatial relationships between objects, best described by a successor representation (predictive map) rather than Euclidean distance ([Garvert 2017](../../raw/summaries/garvert2017_relational_knowledge_maps.md))

- fMRI adaptation in bilateral entorhinal cortex decreased monotonically with link distance on hidden graphs (peak t22 = 4.75 right, t22 = 4.42 left, FWE-corrected); MDS of neural distance matrix recovered actual graph topology (r = 0.65, p = 0.003) ([Garvert 2017](../../raw/summaries/garvert2017_relational_knowledge_maps.md))

- The neural metric for relational knowledge in entorhinal cortex follows successor representation/communicability rather than Euclidean distance; when communicability competed with Euclidean distance, communicability alone predicted entorhinal activity (left hippocampal-entorhinal: FWE p = 0.006) ([Garvert 2017](../../raw/summaries/garvert2017_relational_knowledge_maps.md))
