# Substantia Nigra (pars compacta)

The substantia nigra pars compacta (SNc) is the second major group of dopamine neurons in the midbrain, alongside the ventral tegmental area (VTA). SNc dopamine neurons project primarily to the dorsal striatum and play a critical role in motor control, action selection, and reinforcement learning.

## Key evidence

- Substantia nigra (pars compacta) is the second major group of dopamine neurons with prediction error encoding properties similar to VTA neurons; it sends projections to dorsal striatum ([Schultz 1997](../../raw/summaries/schultz1997_neural_substrate_reward_pred.md))

- Dopamine neurons in both VTA and substantia nigra encode a temporal difference (TD) prediction error signal δ(t) = r(t) + γV̂(t+1) - V̂(t), providing positive signals when outcomes are better than expected, zero when expectations match, and negative when outcomes are worse than expected ([Schultz 1997](../../raw/summaries/schultz1997_neural_substrate_reward_pred.md))
