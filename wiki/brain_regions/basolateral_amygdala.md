# Basolateral Amygdala

The basolateral amygdala (BLA) is a subcortical nucleus critical for emotional learning, memory, and regulation of motivational states. It forms reciprocal connections with the medial prefrontal cortex and other limbic structures.

## Key evidence

### Connectivity with medial prefrontal cortex

- BLA provides L2-enriched input in prelimbic cortex (PL), selectively targeting cortico-amygdalar neurons; this connectivity is critical for emotional encoding, expression, and extinction ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- BLA and ventral hippocampus show graded innervation across ACC, PL, and IL, with distinct laminar and cell-type targeting in each subregion, indicating wiring rules differ along the dorsoventral axis of mPFC ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- Many long-range inputs, including BLA inputs, preferentially contact neurons that project back to the input region, providing a synaptic basis for strong reciprocal loops (e.g., BLA↔mPFC) ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))
