## Key evidence

- RLPFC is not the apex of the frontal hierarchy; anatomical connectional asymmetry meta-analysis and dynamic causal modelling identify mid-DLPFC as having the highest hierarchical strength ([Badre 2018](../../raw/summaries/badre2018_frontal_cortex_hierarchical_control.md))
- RLPFC functions as a domain-specific input region for schematic control, transmitting internally generated schema-based knowledge from vmPFC-hippocampal circuits into the contextual control hierarchy at mid-DLPFC ([Badre 2018](../../raw/summaries/badre2018_frontal_cortex_hierarchical_control.md))
- RLPFC activity ramps across serial positions within task sequences, with single-pulse TMS causing increasing errors at later sequence positions, confirming necessity for sequential control near sequence boundaries ([Badre 2019](../../raw/summaries/badre2019_hierarchical_cognitive_control_lobes.md))
- The ramping RLPFC signal is not modulated by external cue availability or by whether task-switching is required, suggesting it tracks approach to a sequence boundary rather than task uncertainty per se ([Badre 2019](../../raw/summaries/badre2019_hierarchical_cognitive_control_lobes.md))
- RLPFC activation during navigation correlates with hippocampal goal representations, supporting its role in accessing explicit memory-based plans ([Badre 2019](../../raw/summaries/badre2019_hierarchical_cognitive_control_lobes.md))
