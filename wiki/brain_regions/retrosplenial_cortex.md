## Key evidence

- The retrosplenial cortex is a critical hub for memory, navigation, imagination and future planning, consistently activated across virtually all spatial navigation tasks and ubiquitously engaged during episodic/autobiographical memory retrieval ([Vann 2009](../../raw/summaries/vann2009_retrosplenial_cortex_memory.md))

- The RSC achieves its diverse cognitive functions through translation between egocentric and allocentric spatial reference frames, transforming allocentric representations from the hippocampal/medial temporal system into egocentric representations used by parietal and prefrontal regions ([Vann 2009](../../raw/summaries/vann2009_retrosplenial_cortex_memory.md))

- RSC lesions impair spatial memory across multiple tasks (water maze, radial-arm maze, path integration, direction tasks), with effects emerging most strongly when animals must switch between spatial strategies ([Vann 2009](../../raw/summaries/vann2009_retrosplenial_cortex_memory.md))

- The RSC contains head-direction cells (~10% of RSC cells) and generates locally-produced theta activity partially independent of septal and hippocampal inputs; temporary RSC inactivation transiently changes hippocampal place cell spatial tuning ([Vann 2009](../../raw/summaries/vann2009_retrosplenial_cortex_memory.md))

- RSC damage in humans causes selective deficit in spatial orientation with inability to derive directional information from landmarks; unlike hippocampal patients, RSC patients cannot orient in familiar environments and lack sense of direction ([Vann 2009](../../raw/summaries/vann2009_retrosplenial_cortex_memory.md))

- The RSC is consistently compromised in early-stage Alzheimer's disease, with metabolic decline preceding hippocampal atrophy; RSC hypometabolism is found in temporal lobe and diencephalic amnesia ([Vann 2009](../../raw/summaries/vann2009_retrosplenial_cortex_memory.md))

- RSC spatial representations develop progressively across learning stages, with position coding improving early, trajectory coding emerging mid-learning, and selective future-goal coding appearing only at asymptotic performance ([Miller 2019](../../raw/summaries/miller2019_retrosplenial_spatial_learning.md))

- RSC lesions have no effect during early learning but selectively impair asymptotic performance, with lesion size strongly correlated with impairment magnitude (r = -0.90, p < 0.01) ([Miller 2019](../../raw/summaries/miller2019_retrosplenial_spatial_learning.md))

- RSC neurons do not develop hippocampus-like place fields; improved population representations reflect increased reliability of broad firing patterns rather than sharpening of individual tuning curves ([Miller 2019](../../raw/summaries/miller2019_retrosplenial_spatial_learning.md))

- RSC selectively simulates upcoming correct reward locations at asymptotic performance, coinciding with when lesions produce deficits; selective decoding to correct reward area only significant during asymptotic performance (t(14) = 3.48, p < 0.005) ([Miller 2019](../../raw/summaries/miller2019_retrosplenial_spatial_learning.md))

- Trial-type specificity in RSC increases from chance early in learning to 21% of neurons at asymptote; linear classifier on population activity reaches perfect accuracy at asymptote and correlates with within-session performance (F(3,132) = 24.38, p < 0.001) ([Miller 2019](../../raw/summaries/miller2019_retrosplenial_spatial_learning.md))

- NMF applied to rat RSC electrophysiology recovers sparse, parts-based representations of allocentric position, head direction, and route-based movement direction ([Beyeler2019](../../raw/summaries/beyeler2019_sparse_coding_dimensionality.md))

## History of ideas

The RSC was considered a mysterious region with unknown function for decades despite early anatomical description by Brodmann. Early theories focused on emotion and autonomic regulation via the Papez circuit, potentially diverting attention from cognitive functions. Recent advances have established the RSC as a key member of a core brain network underlying episodic memory, navigation, imagination and planning.
