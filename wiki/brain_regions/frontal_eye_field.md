# Frontal Eye Field (FEF)

The frontal eye field (FEF) is a region of prefrontal cortex involved in the control of spatial attention and eye movements. It plays a critical role in coordinating visual attention, temporal expectation, and motor preparation during goal-directed behavior.

## Key evidence

- FEF neural populations simultaneously encode at least four parameters (spatial position, cue-to-target onset asynchrony/CTOA, attentional focus/TA, and reaction time/RT) through mixed-selectivity coding; 72% of recorded FEF neurons showed mixed selectivity, with only 27.2% tuned to a single parameter ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- CTOA (temporal expectation) is encoded in two distinct low-dimensional components with different neural profiles — one linear and one inverted-U shaped; dPCA captured CTOA variance in two orthogonal components: one with linear activation profile (R² = 0.98) correlating with RT, and one with inverted-U profile (R² = 0.97) correlating with TA ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- The CTOA-encoding components are non-orthogonal to both RT and TA components, creating representational overlap that predicts behavioral interactions; dPCA angular analysis showed CTOA components were significantly non-orthogonal to both RT and TA components, with activation of first CTOA dPC correlating with RT across sessions (R² = 0.79) and second CTOA dPC correlating with TA (R² = 0.49) ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- RT and TA components remain orthogonal to each other, preserving independent decodability despite the non-orthogonal overlap with CTOA components ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- Non-orthogonal coding in FEF is a systematic strategy for maximizing encoding capacity under dimensionality constraints, with predictable behavioral costs; non-orthogonality is not a deficiency but a measurable, structured configuration that produces behavioral correlations between CTOA, RT, TA, and hit rate ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- FEF firing rates are significantly modulated by temporal expectation (CTOA), reaction time, and attentional focus with distinct correlational patterns; pre-target FEF firing rate (-200 to 0 ms) was significantly modulated by CTOA (linear, rho = 0.95), RT (linear, rho = 0.92), and TA (quadratic, R² = 0.66) ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))
