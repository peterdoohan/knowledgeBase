# Infralimbic Cortex

The infralimbic cortex (IL) is a ventral subregion of the medial prefrontal cortex in rodents. It is situated ventral to the prelimbic cortex and is strongly interconnected with limbic structures including the ventral hippocampus, basolateral amygdala, and hypothalamus. The IL is implicated in motivation, emotion, and extinction learning.

## Key evidence

### Connectivity patterns

- Infralimbic cortex receives prominent input from ventral hippocampus (vHPC), which primarily targets L5 of IL with weaker input to prelimbic cortex; vHPC preferentially contacts intratelencephalic (IT) cells in IL ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- BLA and vHPC show graded innervation across ACC, PL, and IL, with distinct laminar and cell-type targeting in each subregion, indicating wiring rules differ along the dorsoventral axis of mPFC ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

### Functional implications

- The distinct connectivity profile of IL compared to PL suggests these subregions may serve different functional roles in emotional and motivational processing, though the precise computational differences remain to be established ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))
