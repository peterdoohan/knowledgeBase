## Key evidence

- Mid-DLPFC (areas 45/46) is the apex of the frontal processing hierarchy based on connectional asymmetry meta-analysis and effective connectivity, not RLPFC ([Badre 2018](../../raw/summaries/badre2018_frontal_cortex_hierarchical_control.md))
- Dynamic causal modelling (Nee & D'Esposito 2016) confirms mid-DLPFC as hierarchical apex: hierarchical strength (outward > inward effective connectivity) peaks at mid-DLPFC and drops back at RLPFC to a level analogous to caudal sensory-motor regions ([Badre 2018](../../raw/summaries/badre2018_frontal_cortex_hierarchical_control.md))
- Mid-DLPFC exerts domain-general top-down influence on premotor contextual control regions, receiving domain-specific inputs from caudal areas and schematic input from RLPFC ([Badre 2019](../../raw/summaries/badre2019_hierarchical_cognitive_control_lobes.md))
