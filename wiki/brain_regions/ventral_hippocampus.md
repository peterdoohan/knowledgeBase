# Ventral Hippocampus

The ventral hippocampus (vHPC) is the ventral portion of the hippocampus, distinct from the dorsal hippocampus in both its anatomical connectivity and functional roles. While the dorsal hippocampus is primarily associated with spatial and cognitive processing, the ventral hippocampus is more strongly connected to limbic structures and plays key roles in emotional regulation, stress responses, and contextual/mnemonic control signals to the prefrontal cortex.

## Key evidence

### Connectivity with medial prefrontal cortex

- Ventral hippocampus primarily targets L5 of infralimbic cortex (IL), with weaker input to prelimbic cortex (PL); vHPC preferentially contacts intratelencephalic (IT) cells, providing contextual/mnemonic and emotional control signals ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- vHPC activates CCK+ interneurons that show endocannabinoid-mediated presynaptic modulation specifically at IT cell synapses, adding a neuromodulatory layer of pathway specificity ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- BLA and vHPC show graded innervation across ACC, PL, and IL, with distinct laminar and cell-type targeting in each subregion, indicating wiring rules differ along the dorsoventral axis of mPFC ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

## Open questions

- Whether findings from mouse PL generalise to other subregions, other rodents, or primates remains unresolved ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- The relative functional dominance of feedforward excitation, feedforward inhibition, feedback inhibition, and disinhibition in vivo — and how they combine during behaviour — is unresolved ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))
