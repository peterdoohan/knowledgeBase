## Key evidence

- A scalar dopaminergic surprise signal, evaluated early in the sensory hierarchy but projected to high-level cortical areas including prefrontal cortex, can upregulate learning rates in cortical predictive models ([Akam 2021](../../raw/summaries/akam2021_dopamine_model_based_rl.md))

- Belief instability (κ₁) and response stochasticity (ζ) are co-elevated in schizophrenia and negatively correlated across subjects, consistent with a shared neurobiological origin in unstable attractor dynamics. ([Adams 2018](../../raw/summaries/adams2018_attractor_schizophrenia.md))

- Prelimbic cortex (PL), a subregion of medial prefrontal cortex, provides excitatory input to zona incerta medial (ZIm) that is required for deep investigation of novel stimuli. Chemogenetic silencing of PL→ZIm terminals abolishes deep investigation, reducing investigation duration from ~73s to ~2s. This demonstrates that prefrontal drive to ZIm is necessary for committed investigatory behavior of both objects and conspecifics ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

- The PL signal to ZIm is hypothesized to convey non-specific motivational drive that is gated and thresholded by ZIm based on sensory context. This represents a gain-modulation mechanism where cortical excitation is multiplied by subcortical sensory context before triggering behavioral output. However, it remains unclear whether the PL signal conveys novelty-specific information or general motivational arousal ([Ahmadlou 2021](../../raw/summaries/ahmadlou2021_novelty_seeking_behavior.md)).

### Circuit organization of medial prefrontal cortex

- The mPFC is agranular — it lacks a canonical L4 — so thalamic and other long-range inputs are distributed across L1–L6, unlike sensory cortex where primary thalamic input targets L4. This architectural difference fundamentally changes how the mPFC processes incoming information ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- Projection neurons in mPFC span L2–L6 and fall into three major classes (intratelencephalic/IT, pyramidal tract/PT, corticothalamic/CT), each with distinct morphology, intrinsic physiology, and subcellular properties ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- Long-range inputs to mPFC are laminar- and cell-type-specific: BLA preferentially targets L2 cortico-amygdalar neurons; mediodorsal thalamus strongly drives L3 IT cells; ventromedial thalamus enriches inputs at distal apical dendrites of L5 PT cells; and ventral hippocampus preferentially targets L5 IT cells ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- In local mPFC excitatory circuits, IT cells strongly innervate PT cells (but not vice versa), imposing a directional flow that ensures local computations are funnelled into output pathways ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- mPFC interneurons show biased connectivity preferentially targeting L5 PT cells over IT cells, with PV+, SOM+, CCK+, and NDNF+ interneurons all exhibiting this PT-targeting bias, meaning inhibition is organized to selectively gate output pathways rather than globally suppress activity ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- FEF neural populations (part of prefrontal cortex) simultaneously encode at least four parameters (spatial position, cue-to-target onset asynchrony/CTOA, attentional focus/TA, and reaction time/RT) through mixed-selectivity coding; 72% of recorded FEF neurons showed mixed selectivity, with only 27.2% tuned to a single parameter ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- The CTOA-encoding components in FEF are non-orthogonal to both RT and TA components, challenging the prevailing assumption that mixed-selectivity cells would tend to implement orthogonal representations when possible; this shows non-orthogonality is a structured coding strategy rather than a deficiency ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- Non-orthogonal coding in FEF is a systematic strategy for maximizing encoding capacity under dimensionality constraints, with predictable behavioral costs; non-orthogonality is not a deficiency but a measurable, structured configuration that produces behavioral correlations between temporal expectation, attention, and reaction time ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- Spike-timing-dependent plasticity can implement successor representation learning through prospective coding, consistent with ramping activity observed in prefrontal neurons anticipating predictable stimuli; this provides a biologically plausible mechanism for SR learning in cortical circuits ([Gershman 2018](../../raw/summaries/gershman2018_successor_representation_learning.md))

- Goal neglect — failure to act on a understood and remembered task requirement — is strongly correlated with Spearman's g in the normal population. Correlation between goal neglect and Culture Fair IQ was r = .41, rising to r = .52 in elderly and r = .55 under dual-task conditions; goal neglect showed higher correlation with g than comparably distributed items from the Culture Fair test itself. ([Duncan 1996](../../raw/summaries/duncan1996_intelligence_frontal_goal.md))

- Goal neglect is the predominant deficit following frontal lobe lesions, implicating frontal action-control functions in general intelligence. 7/10 frontal patients showed complete goal neglect vs. 1/10 matched controls; F(1,9) = 14.4, p < .005. Posterior lesion patients showed no deficit. Frontal patients also showed mean 9-point Culture Fair IQ deficit (F(1,9) = 14.8, p < .005). ([Duncan 1996](../../raw/summaries/duncan1996_intelligence_frontal_goal.md))

- Goal neglect reflects failure of goal activation — converting verbally understood requirements into active behavioral controllers — not working memory or verbal ability per se. Verbal prompts eliminated neglect in both normal and frontal subjects; once a subject first correctly used the requirement, performance was almost immediately and durably correct (~1-2% subsequent failures). Many subjects who neglected the requirement reported having seen the symbols, showing stimulus awareness and goal activation are dissociable. ([Duncan 1996](../../raw/summaries/duncan1996_intelligence_frontal_goal.md))

- Concurrent goal requirements increase goal neglect and strengthen its correlation with g, consistent with a limited-capacity goal-activation process. Adding a secondary task increased goal neglect and raised the g correlation; secondary task neglect was much greater and more closely tied to g (r = .66 vs. .19) when instructions were given last, confirming concurrent goal load as a key determinant. ([Duncan 1996](../../raw/summaries/duncan1996_intelligence_frontal_goal.md))

- Fluid intelligence, not crystallized intelligence, predicts goal neglect, dissociating current problem-solving from accumulated knowledge. In elderly subjects, fluid intelligence (Culture Fair) predicted neglect (r ≈ .52) whereas verbal knowledge (Mill Hill r = .02; Nelson-Denny r = .30) did not, showing a specific link to current problem-solving ability rather than accumulated knowledge. ([Duncan 1996](../../raw/summaries/duncan1996_intelligence_frontal_goal.md))

- Right prefrontal cortex lesions specifically impair real-world planning by causing premature commitment to concrete solutions before adequate problem space exploration. RPFC patients produced significantly lower-quality plans than normal controls (F[3,16]=4.76, p=.015); LPFC and posterior groups did not differ from NC. RPFC patients had significantly higher concrete-to-abstract statement ratios (F[3,16]=3.90, p=.029). ([Goel 2013](../../raw/summaries/goel2013_prefrontal_planning_real_world.md))

- Transient inactivations of brain areas that are dispensable for learned behavior (as shown by permanent lesions) can still severely disrupt behavior by acutely perturbing downstream circuits. Muscimol injection into motor cortex severely degraded learned lever-pressing in rats, even though permanent motor cortex lesions leave the skill intact; recovery after lesions requires no task practice and occurs via homeostatic regulation ([Otchy 2015](../../raw/summaries/otchy2015_neural_circuit_motor.md))

- The prefrontal cortex is a critical component of the navigation system, not merely a general executive support system. PFC subregions make distinct contributions: dorsolateral/vlPFC for inhibition and replanning during detours; dACC for hierarchical planning and backtracking; OFC for integrating environmental representations with action values; vmPFC for maintaining current goals during navigation ([Patai 2021](../../raw/summaries/patai2021_versatile_wayfinder_prefrontal.md))

- Rodent medial frontal areas (prelimbic, infralimbic) interact with hippocampus during spatial decisions and strategy switching between place-based and response-based navigation. Coordinated replay between hippocampus and PFC is higher for subsequently taken paths and predicts spatial memory performance; theta-band coherence increases during planning and spatial memory retrieval ([Patai 2021](../../raw/summaries/patai2021_versatile_wayfinder_prefrontal.md))

- Medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC) neurons gradually acquire category selectivity during rule-based visual categorization learning in mice. 9.8% of neurons became category-selective after learning rule 1; Go- and NoGo-preferring populations showed distinct dynamics with Go cells emerging rapidly and remapping after rule-switch, while NoGo cells emerged gradually and were replaced after switch ([Reinert 2021](../../raw/summaries/reinert2021_mouse_prefrontal_categorization.md))

- A sparse subset of mPFC neurons (4.3%) encode categories independently of motor contingencies and reward, providing direct evidence for abstract categorical representation in rodent mPFC. Mice generalized learned rules to novel stimuli upon first presentation, with rule 2 learned significantly faster than rule 1 (p = 9.77 × 10⁻⁴) ([Reinert 2021](../../raw/summaries/reinert2021_mouse_prefrontal_categorization.md))

- Right PFC specifically supports early planning phases requiring abstract representations, while left PFC is not required for real-world planning. The concrete-to-abstract ratio was significantly elevated for RPFC patients in the middle planning phase (F[3,15]=4.28, p=.023). LPFC patients showed no planning deficit, did not differ from NC in time-on-task or abstractness ratios, and were not significantly different from any group. ([Goel 2013](../../raw/summaries/goel2013_prefrontal_planning_real_world.md))

- The right PFC deficit is specific to representational abstractness, not to operator repertoire, episode structure, or planning phase allocation. No significant between-group differences were found in operator distributions, episode types, or allocation of time to problem-solving phases. The deficit was specific to the abstractness dimension - NC showed a 'judicious interplay' of concrete and abstract representations while RPFC patients locked into concrete detail prematurely. ([Goel 2013](../../raw/summaries/goel2013_prefrontal_planning_real_world.md))

- Right PFC patients spend less time on planning tasks but show no deficit in well-structured laboratory tasks like Tower of London. RPFC patients spent significantly less time on the Travel Planning Task than NC (RPFC M=28.75 min vs NC M=58.80 min; F[3,16]=4.31, p=.024). The deficit is specific to ill-structured real-world planning - standard well-structured tasks like Tower of London/Hanoi fail to detect right PFC contributions. ([Goel 2013](../../raw/summaries/goel2013_prefrontal_planning_real_world.md))

- Meta-RL provides a unifying computational account of prefrontal-dopamine interactions. Recurrent deep RL networks trained on task families develop fast in-context learning algorithms in their activation dynamics, where slow weight-level RL shapes activation dynamics for rapid adaptation without further weight changes. This explains PFC activity showing rapid task adaptation inconsistent with synaptic timescales ([Botvinick 2020](../../raw/summaries/botvinick2020_deep_reinforcement_learning_neuro.md))

- Deep RL systems learn task-relevant internal representations shaped by reward signals, not merely by sensory statistics, paralleling neuroscientific evidence that prefrontal representations depend on task and reward contingency. This provides a principled account of reward-shaped representation that neither deep learning alone nor RL alone can provide ([Botvinick 2020](../../raw/summaries/botvinick2020_deep_reinforcement_learning_neuro.md))

- Multi-level RL architectures in deep RL recapitulate features of frontal hierarchy, including cost-of-control mechanisms, automatic versus controlled processing distinctions, and timescale gradients across cortical areas. These architectures provide computational models for how frontal cortex might organize behavior hierarchically ([Botvinick 2020](../../raw/summaries/botvinick2020_deep_reinforcement_learning_neuro.md))

- The prefrontal cortex is a critical component of the navigation system, not merely a general executive support system; synthesizes evidence across humans, non-human primates, and rodents showing PFC involvement in navigation, with patients with vmPFC damage showing specific navigation deficits beyond general executive dysfunction ([Patai 2021](../../raw/summaries/patai2021_versatile_wayfinder_prefrontal.md))

- The prefrontal cortex, not the hippocampus, shows consistent increased activity during forced detours in navigation; all 9 neuroimaging studies reviewed reported increased PFC activity during detour conditions, while hippocampus showed no consistent increase and two studies found decreased activity ([Spiers 2015](../../raw/summaries/spiers2015_detour_problem_navigation.md))

- Frontopolar PFC (BA 10) is the most consistently activated region during detours and supports subgoal generation and goal-subgoal integration; most consistently activated region across 9 studies, implicated in subgoal generation and goal-subgoal integration during route replanning ([Spiers 2015](../../raw/summaries/spiers2015_detour_problem_navigation.md))

- Lateral PFC detects model-based prediction errors when expected routes are blocked and initiates route replanning; activated in response to unexpected environmental changes requiring route adjustment, proposed to signal model-based prediction errors about route expectations ([Spiers 2015](../../raw/summaries/spiers2015_detour_problem_navigation.md))

- Superior PFC (BA 6) resolves conflict between alternative route options during detour planning; active during conflict between alternative route options, may support conflict resolution during multi-route planning ([Spiers 2015](../../raw/summaries/spiers2015_detour_problem_navigation.md))

- Dorsolateral PFC and ventrolateral PFC support inhibition and replanning during detours and environmental changes; active during detours and environmental changes requiring inhibition and replanning, dlPFC shows increased activity during active route choice compared to guided navigation ([Patai 2021](../../raw/summaries/patai2021_versatile_wayfinder_prefrontal.md))

- Orbitofrontal cortex integrates environmental representations with action values for value-based navigation decisions; integrates environmental representations with action values, supports inference about hidden states and value-based decision-making at choice points, in rodents OFC inactivation abolishes inference-based foraging while preserving stimulus-bound behavior ([Patai 2021](../../raw/summaries/patai2021_versatile_wayfinder_prefrontal.md))

- Ventromedial PFC is critical for maintaining current goals during navigation; patients with vmPFC damage can navigate familiar routes but frequently forget their destination goal, performance improves with external reminders, involved in scene construction and integrating new spatial information with existing schemas ([Patai 2021](../../raw/summaries/patai2021_versatile_wayfinder_prefrontal.md))

- Prefrontal cortex delay-period activity exhibits diffusive bump dynamics on a 1-dimensional manifold, consistent with continuous attractor working memory models. Bump displacement predicts behavioural error in spatial working memory tasks; population activity flows on a low-dimensional manifold. ([Khona 2022](../../raw/summaries/khona2022_attractor_integrator.md))

- Agranular orbitofrontal cortex (OFC) and anterior cingulate cortex (ACC) connectivity with striatum, amygdala, hippocampus, hypothalamus, and thalamus is largely conserved across rat and macaque, with anterior-posterior parcellation providing the best cross-species alignment rather than dorsal-ventral organization. ([Izquierdo 2024](../../raw/summaries/izquierdo2024_prefrontal_cortex_homology.md))

- Rodent frontal cortex is completely agranular; primate prefrontal cortex uniquely contains granular areas (e.g., lateral and dorsolateral PFC, area 46) that support visual representations of "valueless" reward unavailable in rodents, limiting cross-species translation for certain cognitive functions. ([Izquierdo 2024](../../raw/summaries/izquierdo2024_prefrontal_cortex_homology.md))

- Rat frontal cortex subregions show less functional specialization than primate OFC and ACC, exhibiting redundant theta-band signals during reversal learning and both impairing confidence reporting when inhibited, contrasting with the OFC/ACC division of labor for stimuli vs. actions seen in primates. ([Izquierdo 2024](../../raw/summaries/izquierdo2024_prefrontal_cortex_homology.md))

- Primate cortical evolution favored clustering of cell types; mouse Layer 3 pyramidal neurons are morphologically homogeneous across areas, whereas macaque Layer 3 lateral PFC neurons are substantially larger with different dendritic topology compared to macaque V1, suggesting species-specific microcircuit specializations. ([Izquierdo 2024](../../raw/summaries/izquierdo2024_prefrontal_cortex_homology.md))

- Prefrontal cortex exhibits a rostro-caudal gradient where progressively more abstract behavioral representations are encoded more anteriorly, with hierarchical RL providing a computational framework for this organization ([Botvinick 2008](../../raw/summaries/botvinick2008_hierarchical_behavior_prefrontal.md))

- The prefrontal cortex is implicated in homeostatic plasticity mechanisms; dysbindin, a schizophrenia-associated gene, has been implicated in presynaptic homeostasis through forward genetic screening, linking PFC-related genes to homeostatic signaling disorders ([Davis 2013](../../raw/summaries/davis2013_homeostatic_signaling_neural_stabilization.md))

- TOR/S6K signaling, which is required for sustained maintenance of presynaptic homeostasis, links metabolic sensing to homeostatic effector expression in neural circuits including those involving prefrontal function ([Davis 2013](../../raw/summaries/davis2013_homeostatic_signaling_neural_stabilization.md))

- Dorsolateral prefrontal cortex houses option representations (task sets as temporally abstract policies) rather than merely transient working-memory buffers, according to the hierarchical RL framework ([Botvinick 2008](../../raw/summaries/botvinick2008_hierarchical_behavior_prefrontal.md))

- Prefrontal cortex neurons code for key decision variables required for efficient learning including current value, last action, last reward, and action-reward interactions, as demonstrated by meta-RL models matching primate dlPFC neural coding patterns ([Botvinick 2019](../../raw/summaries/botvinick2019_reinforcement_learning_fast_slow.md))

- Model-based information can emerge in ventral striatal prediction errors through meta-RL training without explicit model-based architecture, providing a unified account of prefrontal-dopamine interactions ([Botvinick 2019](../../raw/summaries/botvinick2019_reinforcement_learning_fast_slow.md))

- A subset of PFC neurons is selectively tuned to the content of hippocampal replay rather than the animal's actual location. 18.5% of PFC neurons (28/151; p = 2.2e-9) were significantly differentially modulated by replay of different maze arms; decoding scaled with number of recorded PFC neurons. ([Berners-Lee 2021](../../raw/summaries/bernerslee2021_prefrontal_cortex_hippocampal.md))

- PFC replay selectivity is stronger than what could be predicted from behavioral arm selectivity. Simulated reactivation responses derived from firing rates during running were significantly less arm-selective than observed responses (Wilcoxon signed-rank, p = 9.8e-4), ruling out simple reactivation of behavioral patterns. ([Berners-Lee 2021](../../raw/summaries/bernerslee2021_prefrontal_cortex_hippocampal.md))

- PFC neurons track non-local hippocampal representations during both rest (replay) and behavior (theta sequences). PFC neurons were more strongly driven by HP spikes from late (non-local) half of theta cycles than early (local) half (p = 4.9e-5); PFC arm-modulation patterns matched non-local HP representations better than local (p = 0.0197). ([Berners-Lee 2021](../../raw/summaries/bernerslee2021_prefrontal_cortex_hippocampal.md))

- Non-local PFC activity predicts upcoming choice better than local activity. PFC spikes locked to non-local theta epochs decoded future choice above chance (p < 0.001), while PFC spikes locked to local epochs did not (p > 0.8); non-local decoding significantly outperformed local (p < 1e-4). ([Berners-Lee 2021](../../raw/summaries/bernerslee2021_prefrontal_cortex_hippocampal.md))

- Meta-learning provides a unifying computational account of prefrontal-dopamine interactions. Recurrent deep RL networks trained on task families develop fast in-context learning algorithms in their activation dynamics, where slow weight-level RL shapes activation dynamics for rapid adaptation without further weight changes. This explains PFC activity showing rapid task adaptation inconsistent with synaptic timescales. ([Binz 2024](../../raw/summaries/binz2024_meta_learning_cognition.md))

- Meta-learning handles 'large world' problems where Bayesian inference cannot be posed because prior and likelihood are unknown. Requires only samples from environment, not explicit prior or likelihood; extends rational analysis to problems where correct Bayesian model is unspecifiable. ([Binz 2024](../../raw/summaries/binz2024_meta_learning_cognition.md))

- Resource-rational models emerge naturally from constraining network size in meta-learning. Limiting weights constrains algorithmic complexity (Kolmogorov-like); limiting activations constrains computational complexity; produces human-like cognitive biases (conservatism, base-rate neglect) and heuristics (single-cue, equal-weighting). ([Binz 2024](../../raw/summaries/binz2024_meta_learning_cognition.md))

- Meta-reinforcement learning maps onto prefrontal-dopaminergic circuitry. Slow synaptic changes (outer loop ~ dopamine-driven) give rise to fast within-activity-dynamics learning (inner loop ~ PFC dynamics); predicts dopamine signals should reflect inferred (not just experienced) reward values consistent with Bromberg-Martin et al. 2010. ([Binz 2024](../../raw/summaries/binz2024_meta_learning_cognition.md))

- MERFISH spatial transcriptomics reveals 52 distinct cell subtypes in mouse PFC organized in 3D anatomical space ([Bhattacherjee2022](../../raw/summaries/bhattacherjee2022_prefrontal_spatial_transcriptomics.md))

- PFC shows unique ion-channel gene expression signature with enrichment of voltage-gated potassium channels and AMPA receptor subunits ([Bhattacherjee2022](../../raw/summaries/bhattacherjee2022_prefrontal_spatial_transcriptomics.md))

- L5 ET1 neurons (marked by Pou3f1) projecting to PAG are preferentially deactivated in chronic neuropathic pain ([Bhattacherjee2022](../../raw/summaries/bhattacherjee2022_prefrontal_spatial_transcriptomics.md))
