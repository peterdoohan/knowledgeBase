## Key evidence

- Dorsolateral striatum is the proposed locus of model-free TD learning over punctate states; lesions here preserve devaluation sensitivity even after overtraining, consistent with habit formation ([Russek 2017](../../raw/summaries/russek2017_model_based_reinforcement.md))
- Dorsolateral striatum does not show transient forward sweeps or covert reward signals during VTE; instead, it develops task-bracketing activity (start/end firing) as behavior automates — the procedural system signature ([Redish 2016](../../raw/summaries/redish2016_vicarious_trial_error.md))
- The transition from VTE to automation reflects a shift from hippocampal-PFC-driven deliberation to dorsolateral striatal task-bracketing, establishing a dual-systems dissociation ([Redish 2016](../../raw/summaries/redish2016_vicarious_trial_error.md))

- Dorsolateral striatum is proposed to implement option-specific action strengths (policies) that integrate current option identity from frontal cortex with environmental state to select primitive actions or sub-options, corresponding to Extension 2 of the HRL actor ([Botvinick 2009](../../raw/summaries/botvinick2009_hierarchical_behavior_reinforcement.md))

- DLS showed faster dynamics (shorter time to minimum) compared to lNAc in TD computation experiments, with gamma numerically closer to 1, suggesting region-specific tuning of temporal discounting parameters ([Campbell 2025](../../raw/summaries/campbell2025_hardwired_circuit_td_learning.md))

- Biphasic impulse responses (TD transfer functions) were observed for D1-MSN stimulation in DLS, indicating the hardwired TD computation motif is conserved across striatal subregions with region-specific parameters ([Campbell 2025](../../raw/summaries/campbell2025_hardwired_circuit_td_learning.md))
