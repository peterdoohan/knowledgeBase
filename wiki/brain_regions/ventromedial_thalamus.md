# Ventromedial Thalamus

The ventromedial thalamus (VM) is a higher-order thalamic nucleus that provides dense input to layer 1a of the prefrontal cortex. It is distinct from the mediodorsal thalamus in both its anatomical targets and functional roles, particularly in arousal and cortical state regulation.

## Key evidence

### Connectivity with medial prefrontal cortex

- Ventromedial thalamus provides dense L1a input to mPFC and selectively targets pyramidal tract (PT) apical dendrites; this enrichment at distal apical dendrites is a motif not seen in sensory cortex and points to a distinct mechanism for modulating mPFC output ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- VM drives neurogliaform (NDNF+) cells in L1a, showing specific recruitment of distinct interneuron populations compared to other thalamic inputs ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- VM thalamic inputs to PT apical tufts can evoke dendritic Ca²⁺ spikes, analogous to perforant path gating in hippocampal CA1 — a mechanism for multiplicative gating or coincidence detection at the single-cell level ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

## Open questions

- Whether findings from mouse PL generalise to other subregions, other rodents, or primates remains unresolved ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- The functional implications of VM input to PT apical dendrites — particularly for synaptic plasticity — are not yet established ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))
