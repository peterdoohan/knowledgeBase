# Lateral Entorhinal Cortex (LEC)

The lateral entorhinal cortex (LEC) is a parahippocampal region that provides direct cortical input to the hippocampus, traditionally associated with non-spatial, item-specific, and contextual information processing.

## Key evidence

- LEC and MEC direct inputs to CA1 carry distinct navigational signals: LEC representations are goal-biased and remap with goal relocation, while MEC provides stable context-specific codes; two-photon axon imaging in CA1 SLM showed LEC remapped within one lap of goal change (11.7% tracked reward vs 3.5% MEC, p=0.039) ([Bowler 2023](../../raw/summaries/bowler2023_cortical_inputs_hippocampal_ca1.md))

- LEC axons show higher spatial tuning in reward-proximal regions (50 cm preceding reward zone) across all tasks, while MEC maintains stable position coding independent of goals; goal-enrichment index significantly higher for LEC; MEC position decoding remained stable across reward conditions ([Bowler 2023](../../raw/summaries/bowler2023_cortical_inputs_hippocampal_ca1.md))

- LEC-CA1 projection is causally necessary for novel reward-location learning, while MEC-CA1 silencing produces no measurable deficit; retrograde-Cre + hM4D DREADD experiments showed LEC silencing impaired learning (p<=0.001 for all DCZ days); MEC silencing showed no effect vs tdTomato controls ([Bowler 2023](../../raw/summaries/bowler2023_cortical_inputs_hippocampal_ca1.md))

- LEC representations are more stable than MEC during context changes but remap with goal translocation; following complete visual context change (fixed goal), MEC axons remapped strongly while LEC representations were more stable, especially near reward zone; more MEC ROIs had context-specific tuning (p=0.0003); more LEC ROIs had reward-distance tuning (p=0.0003) ([Bowler 2023](../../raw/summaries/bowler2023_cortical_inputs_hippocampal_ca1.md))

- LEC loses spatial specificity when goals change every lap (shuffled reward task), while MEC maintains higher spatial stability; LEC showed reduced spatial tuning, lower PV correlations, and worse position decoding when reward location randomized each lap; MEC maintained higher PV correlations, more tuned ROIs, and better position decoding ([Bowler 2023](../../raw/summaries/bowler2023_cortical_inputs_hippocampal_ca1.md))
