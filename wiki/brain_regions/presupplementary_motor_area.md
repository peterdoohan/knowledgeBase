## Key evidence

- Pre-SMA is co-activated with RLPFC during sequential tasks and shows ramping signals during sequence monitoring, supporting its role in sequential control ([Badre 2019](../../raw/summaries/badre2019_hierarchical_cognitive_control_lobes.md))
- A parallel rostrocaudal gradient in medial frontal cortex tracks motivational/conflict signals (caudal SMA → pre-SMA → dorsal ACC), modulating the intensity of control in corresponding lateral PFC zones ([Badre 2018](../../raw/summaries/badre2018_frontal_cortex_hierarchical_control.md))
