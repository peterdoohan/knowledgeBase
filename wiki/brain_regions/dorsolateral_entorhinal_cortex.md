## Key evidence

- The dorsolateral band of the entorhinal cortex is necessary for recall of spatial memory; rats with dorsolateral band lesions spent only 8.7% ± 2.9% of search time in platform zone vs. 38.0% ± 3.0% for sham controls, with 13 of 14 rats performing at chance level ([Steffenach 2005](../../raw/summaries/steffenach2005_spatial_memory_entorhinal.md))

- The dorsolateral band receives visuospatial input from the postrhinal cortex and projects primarily to the dorsal hippocampus, which is critical for spatial processing ([Steffenach 2005](../../raw/summaries/steffenach2005_spatial_memory_entorhinal.md))

- New spatial learning is partially spared after dorsolateral entorhinal lesions that abolish memory retention; during reversal training, escape latencies were longer but final probe trial showed no significant group × quadrant interaction ([Steffenach 2005](../../raw/summaries/steffenach2005_spatial_memory_entorhinal.md))
