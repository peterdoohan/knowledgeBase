## Key evidence

- Adult mice can spontaneously re-express coordinated hindlimb locomotion within 14 days of complete thoracic spinal cord transection without pharmacological treatment, demonstrating that the mouse lumbar spinal cord contains a functional central pattern generator ([Leblond2003](../../raw/summaries/leblond2003_locomotion_spinal.md)).

- 10 of 13 spinal mice recovered locomotion after T8 transection; recovery followed stereotyped sequence with intralimb coordination restored by day 12 and interlimb alternation by day 14 ([Leblond2003](../../raw/summaries/leblond2003_locomotion_spinal.md)).

- Double transection at T8 did not disrupt recovery, ruling out axonal regrowth; establishes lumbar spinal CPG operates autonomously without supraspinal input ([Leblond2003](../../raw/summaries/leblond2003_locomotion_spinal.md)).

- Intralimb coordination recovers before interlimb alternation, suggesting hierarchical organization of spinal locomotor networks with limb-specific rhythm generators becoming functional before coupling circuits ([Leblond2003](../../raw/summaries/leblond2003_locomotion_spinal.md)).

- Step cycle duration in spinal mice significantly longer than intact (700±64 ms vs 500±94 ms, p<0.0005) due to prolonged stance rather than swing phase ([Leblond2003](../../raw/summaries/leblond2003_locomotion_spinal.md)).
