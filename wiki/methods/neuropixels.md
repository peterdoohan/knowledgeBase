# Neuropixels Probes

## Current understanding

Neuropixels probes are high-density silicon probes that enable large-scale single-unit recordings with hundreds of simultaneously recorded neurons across the full depth of the neocortex. The technology has revolutionized systems neuroscience in animal models and is increasingly being adapted for human neurosurgical applications.

## Key evidence

- Neuropixels probes can record 200+ simultaneously active single units in human cortex during intraoperative neurosurgery; successfully recorded 201 ± 151 isolatable units per session with best single-session yield of 312 units in awake dlPFC ([Paulk 2022](../../raw/summaries/paulk2022_neuropixels_human_cortex.md))

- Neuropixels probes achieve 100+ simultaneously active single units across cortical depth in human neurosurgical patients; 596 putative single units isolated across 11 recordings from 8 participants with best single-session yield of 94 units ([Chung 2022](../../raw/summaries/chung2022_neural_recording_neuropixels.md))

- Brain pulsation from cardiac and respiratory rhythms causes 80-100 μm tissue motion in human recordings, requiring post-hoc motion correction for stable unit isolation; post-hoc manual correction reduced motion to 20-40 μm (sub-cellular scale) ([Paulk 2022](../../raw/summaries/paulk2022_neuropixels_human_cortex.md))

- Electrode-brain relative motion is the primary limiting factor for unit yield in human Neuropixels recordings; mean electrode motion was 251 ± 112 μm across recordings, negatively correlated with single-unit yield (Spearman R = -0.62, p = 0.040) ([Chung 2022](../../raw/summaries/chung2022_neural_recording_neuropixels.md))

- Anesthesia significantly delays latency to first spike but does not affect overall unit yield in human recordings; 50th percentile latency: 18.4 s anesthetized vs 3.3 s non-anesthetized (p = 0.014) ([Chung 2022](../../raw/summaries/chung2022_neural_recording_neuropixels.md))

- High-density spatial sampling enables classification of 8 separable single-unit classes with distinct waveform features, firing rates, and laminar distributions; positive spike units had wider waveforms (494.2 μs vs 349.6 μs) and higher firing rates (1.1 Hz vs 0.3-0.4 Hz) ([Paulk 2022](../../raw/summaries/paulk2022_neuropixels_human_cortex.md))

- Temporally coordinated cell pairs are spatially closer than anti-correlated pairs in human cortex, replicating primate findings; cell pairs with positive cell-pair coordination index (CCI) were significantly closer spatially than those with negative CCI ([Chung 2022](../../raw/summaries/chung2022_neural_recording_neuropixels.md))
