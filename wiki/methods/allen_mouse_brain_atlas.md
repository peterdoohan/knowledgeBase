# Allen Mouse Brain Common Coordinate Framework (CCFv3)

## Current understanding

The Allen Mouse Brain Common Coordinate Framework version 3 (CCFv3) is a 3D reference atlas of the mouse brain at 10 μm cellular resolution, constructed from 1,675 mice. It provides a standard spatial reference for integrating multimodal datasets across spatial scales and brain areas, overcoming limitations of previous 2D plate-based atlases and lower-resolution 3D atlases.

## Key evidence

- The Allen Mouse Brain Common Coordinate Framework v3 provides the first cellular-resolution 10 μm isotropic 3D reference atlas for the mouse brain; constructed from 1,675 mice using iterative symmetric diffeomorphic registration; final template at 10 μm isotropic voxels (1,320 × 1,140 × 800 voxels; ~506 million voxels) ([Wang 2020](../../raw/summaries/wang2020_allen_mouse_brain_atlas.md))

- The atlas parcellates the mouse brain into 658 structures including 43 isocortical areas with 6 layers, 329 subcortical gray matter structures, 81 fiber tracts, and 8 ventricular structures; direct 3D annotation using ITK-SNAP with structures drawn in coronal, sagittal, and horizontal planes simultaneously; 36 newly delineated structures not present in previous atlases ([Wang 2020](../../raw/summaries/wang2020_allen_mouse_brain_atlas.md))

- Curved cortical coordinates enable accurate columnar/streamline-based analysis of the isocortex using Laplace's equation; Laplace's equation solved between pial surface and layer 6b/white matter boundary to create streamlines approximating columnar organization; 6 layers delineated across all isocortical areas using layer-specific Cre lines ([Wang 2020](../../raw/summaries/wang2020_allen_mouse_brain_atlas.md))
