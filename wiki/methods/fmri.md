# fMRI

Functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) is a neuroimaging technique that measures brain activity by detecting changes in blood flow and blood oxygenation.

## Key evidence

- vmPFC exhibits a longer hemodynamic response function for prediction error signals compared to ventral striatum, peaking at approximately 8.5 seconds versus 6 seconds post-outcome. This suggests region-specific hemodynamic dynamics or processing delays that may affect detection of vmPFC effects in standard fMRI analyses ([Baram 2021](../../raw/summaries/baram2021_entorhinal_ventromedial_rl.md))
