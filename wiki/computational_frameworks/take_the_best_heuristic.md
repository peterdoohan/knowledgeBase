## Key evidence

- Macaque monkeys trained to play Pac-Man use a "take-the-best" (TTB) heuristic where the dominant strategy weight (0.907±0.117) is significantly larger than secondary (0.273±0.233) and tertiary (0.087±0.137) strategies ([Yang2022](../../raw/summaries/yang2022_monkey_pacman_strategy.md)).

- In over 90% of time points, the weight difference between first and second strategies exceeded 0.1; in 33% of cases it exceeded 0.9, demonstrating strong dominance of a single strategy at each decision point ([Yang2022](../../raw/summaries/yang2022_monkey_pacman_strategy.md)).

- Monkeys use hierarchical decision-making with three-level hierarchy: (1) action selection, (2) basis strategy selection, and (3) compound strategy assembly, with the take-the-best heuristic simplifying computational complexity ([Yang2022](../../raw/summaries/yang2022_monkey_pacman_strategy.md)).
