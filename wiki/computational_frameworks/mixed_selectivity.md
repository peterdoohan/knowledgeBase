# Mixed Selectivity

Mixed selectivity refers to the property of neural populations where individual neurons encode multiple task parameters or variables simultaneously. This coding strategy enables high-dimensional representations and flexible computation in neural circuits, particularly in prefrontal cortex.

## Key evidence

- FEF neural populations simultaneously encode at least four parameters (spatial position, cue-to-target onset asynchrony/CTOA, attentional focus/TA, and reaction time/RT) through mixed-selectivity coding; 72% of recorded FEF neurons showed mixed selectivity, with only 27.2% tuned to a single parameter ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- The CTOA-encoding components are non-orthogonal to both RT and TA components, creating representational overlap that predicts behavioral interactions; this challenges the prevailing assumption that mixed-selectivity cells would tend to implement orthogonal representations when possible, showing non-orthogonality is a structured coding strategy rather than a deficiency ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- Non-orthogonal coding in FEF is a systematic strategy for maximizing encoding capacity under dimensionality constraints, with predictable behavioral costs; non-orthogonality is not a deficiency but a measurable, structured configuration that produces behavioral correlations between temporal expectation, attention, and reaction time ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- Primate prefrontal cortex has significantly higher proportions of interneurons relative to V1 than rodent, with more specialized spatial expression gradients for interneuron marker genes (Sst, Pvalb, Vip, Lamp5) across PFC vs. V1 — a specialization in expression, not origin. ([Izquierdo 2024](../../raw/summaries/izquierdo2024_prefrontal_cortex_homology.md))

- Gene regulatory elements in glutamatergic and GABAergic cell subtypes diverge more across species than shared-type GREs, with the most divergent gene families being neurotransmitter receptors (especially serotonin), ion channels, and cell adhesion molecules — likely impacting microcircuit function and possibly contributing to translation failures in preclinical work. ([Izquierdo 2024](../../raw/summaries/izquierdo2024_prefrontal_cortex_homology.md))
