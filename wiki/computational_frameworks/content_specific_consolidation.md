## Key evidence

- Closed-loop optogenetic disruption of content-specific hippocampal reactivation during sleep selectively impairs recall of the disrupted memories while leaving other memories intact, demonstrating that the memory content of hippocampal reactivation determines which memories are consolidated ([Gridchyn2020](../../raw/summaries/gridchyn2020_replay_selective_memory.md)).

- Place maps destabilized by targeted replay disruption can re-emerge after relearning, suggesting that sleep reactivation consolidates the association between a hippocampal map and the appropriate learning context rather than the map itself ([Gridchyn2020](../../raw/summaries/gridchyn2020_replay_selective_memory.md)).

- Disrupting one memory trace via targeted replay disruption does not degrade other related memories, arguing against broader hippocampal circuit reorganization triggered by replay of related content ([Gridchyn2020](../../raw/summaries/gridchyn2020_replay_selective_memory.md)).
