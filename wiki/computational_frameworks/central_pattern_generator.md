## Key evidence

- The mouse lumbar spinal cord contains a functional central pattern generator for locomotion that operates autonomously without supraspinal input or pharmacological drive ([Leblond2003](../../raw/summaries/leblond2003_locomotion_spinal.md)).

- Adult mice can spontaneously re-express coordinated hindlimb locomotion within 14 days of complete thoracic spinal cord transection, demonstrating intrinsic CPG capacity ([Leblond2003](../../raw/summaries/leblond2003_locomotion_spinal.md)).

- Intralimb coordination recovers before interlimb alternation following spinal transection, suggesting hierarchical organization within spinal locomotor networks ([Leblond2003](../../raw/summaries/leblond2003_locomotion_spinal.md)).
