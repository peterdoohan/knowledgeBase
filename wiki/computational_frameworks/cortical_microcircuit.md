## Key evidence

- Increased self-inhibition of superficial pyramidal cells—reflecting reduced synaptic gain—is the consistent circuit abnormality across resting-state EEG, MMN, 40-Hz ASSR, and rsfMRI in schizophrenia. ([Adams 2021](../../raw/summaries/adams2021_pyramidal_synaptic_gain.md))
- Pyramidal cell synaptic gain loss is a primary deficit in schizophrenia, whereas interneuron pathology is likely a secondary compensatory response rather than a primary cause. ([Adams 2021](../../raw/summaries/adams2021_pyramidal_synaptic_gain.md))
- Compensatory interneuron downregulation (disinhibition) may destabilise cortical circuit dynamics, creating a narrow therapeutic window for glutamatergic treatments that restore pyramidal gain without addressing the compensatory rebalancing. ([Adams 2021](../../raw/summaries/adams2021_pyramidal_synaptic_gain.md))

### Cell-type-specific wiring in medial prefrontal cortex

- The mPFC is agranular — it lacks a canonical L4 — so thalamic and other long-range inputs are distributed across L1–L6, unlike sensory cortex where primary thalamic input targets L4. This architectural difference fundamentally distinguishes mPFC microcircuit organization from the canonical sensory cortical model ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- Projection neurons in mPFC span L2–L6 and fall into three major classes (intratelencephalic/IT, pyramidal tract/PT, corticothalamic/CT), each with distinct morphology, intrinsic physiology, and subcellular properties; this classification provides a more granular framework than the traditional pyramidal cell model ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- In local mPFC excitatory circuits, IT cells strongly innervate PT cells (but not vice versa), imposing a directional flow that ensures local computations are funnelled into output pathways; this IT→PT directionality formalises a processing stage between input integration and output generation ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- mPFC interneurons show biased connectivity preferentially targeting L5 PT cells over IT cells, with PV+, SOM+, CCK+, and NDNF+ interneurons all exhibiting this PT-targeting bias, meaning inhibition is organized to selectively gate output pathways rather than globally suppress activity ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))

- Disinhibitory circuits exist in mPFC: VIP+ cells target SOM+ cells; NDNF+ cells in L1 target PV+ cells in L2/3; these allow long-range inputs to gate inhibitory tone in a pathway-specific manner, providing a circuit mechanism for context-dependent regulation of mPFC output ([Anastasiades 2021](../../raw/summaries/anastasiades2021_circuit_medial_prefrontal.md))
