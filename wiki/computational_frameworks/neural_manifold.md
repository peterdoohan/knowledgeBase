# Neural Manifold

The neural manifold framework conceptualizes population neural activity as occupying a low-dimensional subspace (manifold) within the high-dimensional space of possible firing rates. The geometry of this manifold — including the angles between parameter-specific components — constrains neural computation and predicts behavioral patterns.

## Key evidence

- CTOA (temporal expectation) is encoded in two distinct low-dimensional components with different neural profiles — one linear and one inverted-U shaped; dPCA captured CTOA variance in two orthogonal components: one with linear activation profile (R² = 0.98) correlating with RT, and one with inverted-U profile (R² = 0.97) correlating with TA, demonstrating that multiple components can encode a single parameter within the manifold ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- The CTOA-encoding components are non-orthogonal to both RT and TA components, creating representational overlap that predicts behavioral interactions; dPCA angular analysis showed CTOA components were significantly non-orthogonal to both RT and TA components, with activation of first CTOA dPC correlating with RT across sessions (R² = 0.79) and second CTOA dPC correlating with TA (R² = 0.49) ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- RT and TA components remain orthogonal to each other, preserving independent decodability despite the non-orthogonal overlap with CTOA components; dPCA simultaneous analysis across all parameters showed selective orthogonality within the manifold geometry ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))

- Non-orthogonal coding in FEF is a systematic strategy for maximizing encoding capacity under dimensionality constraints, with predictable behavioral costs; the geometric arrangement produces behavioral correlations between temporal expectation, attention, and reaction time, establishing a formal link between manifold geometry and overt behavior ([Amengual 2022](../../raw/summaries/amengual2022_pfc_multiplexing_behavior.md))
